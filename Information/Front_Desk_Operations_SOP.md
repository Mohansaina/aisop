## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Front Desk Operations Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is to establish a consistent, measurable, and repeatable set of processes governing all Front Desk activities, including guest arrival, guest departure, in-house guest servicing, cashiering, communication handling, and shift transitions. This document ensures that every Front Desk Agent, Guest Service Agent, Front Office Supervisor, and Duty Manager performs their duties in alignment with the property's service standards, brand expectations, and operational compliance requirements.

This SOP also serves as the authoritative reference for the property's AI Operations Assistant knowledge base, allowing employees to retrieve accurate, standardized answers to operational questions related to Front Desk functions at any time.

---

## 2. Scope

This SOP applies to all employees who work at or supervise the Front Desk, including but not limited to:

- Front Desk Agents
- Guest Service Agents
- Front Office Supervisors
- Duty Managers
- Front Office Managers
- Overnight/Night Audit Agents
- Bell Staff and Concierge personnel who interact directly with Front Desk operations

This SOP covers all Front Desk touchpoints including check-in, check-out, telephone operations, cashiering, key control, guest inquiries, complaint handling at the desk, and shift handover procedures. This SOP does not cover Housekeeping room servicing procedures, Reservations department booking procedures, or Food & Beverage service standards, which are addressed in separate departmental SOPs.

## 3. Definitions

- **Front Desk (also referred to as "Front Office," "Reception," or "Guest Services Desk")**: The physical and operational hub where guests check in, check out, and receive service assistance during their stay.
- **Duty Manager**: The manager on duty responsible for overall hotel operations during a given shift, with authority to make guest service and operational decisions in the absence of department heads.
- **PMS (Property Management System)**: The software platform used to manage reservations, guest profiles, room assignments, billing, and check-in/check-out processing.
- **Rate Code**: An alphanumeric code within the PMS identifying the pricing category applied to a guest's reservation.
- **Walk-In Guest**: A guest who arrives without a prior reservation and requests same-day accommodation.
- **Overbooking (also called "Oversold Situation")**: A condition where confirmed reservations exceed available room inventory for a given night.
- **Walking a Guest**: The practice of relocating a confirmed, arriving guest to another property because the original hotel cannot accommodate them due to an oversold situation.
- **Late Checkout (also referred to as "Extended Stay," "Checkout Extension," or "Delayed Departure")**: An approved arrangement allowing a guest to remain in their room beyond the standard checkout time.
- **Early Check-In**: An approved arrangement allowing a guest to occupy their room before the standard check-in time.
- **Key Control**: The set of procedures governing the issuance, tracking, deactivation, and security of guest room keys.
- **Night Audit**: The end-of-day process reconciling all financial transactions, room statuses, and system data to close out the business day.
- **VIP Guest**: A guest designated for elevated service due to loyalty tier, reservation type, executive status, or special company designation.
- **Do Not Disturb (DND)**: A guest-activated status indicating the guest does not wish to be contacted or have their room entered.
- **Trace / Alert**: A PMS-generated note or reminder attached to a reservation requiring specific action by staff (e.g., "confirm airport transfer," "deliver amenity").
- **Folio**: The itemized account record of charges and payments associated with a guest's stay.
- **Rate Discrepancy**: A mismatch between the rate quoted to the guest and the rate reflected in the PMS.

## 4. Roles & Responsibilities

### Front Desk Agent
- Greets and checks in/out guests according to established standards.
- Verifies guest identification and payment method.
- Assigns rooms according to guest preferences and availability.
- Handles cash, credit card, and folio transactions accurately.
- Responds to guest inquiries in person and by telephone.
- Escalates unresolved issues to the Supervisor or Duty Manager.
- Maintains key control procedures.
- Completes shift checklists and handover logs.

### Front Office Supervisor
- Oversees Front Desk Agents during assigned shifts.
- Approves rate overrides, comp adjustments, and room changes within authorized limits.
- Handles escalated guest complaints beyond Agent authority.
- Monitors arrivals/departures reports and oversold situations.
- Conducts spot checks on cash drawers and documentation compliance.
- Trains new Front Desk Agents on procedures.

### Duty Manager
- Holds property-wide authority during assigned shift.
- Makes final decisions on guest compensation, walking guests, and service recovery.
- Responds to emergencies, security incidents, and escalated complaints.
- Approves exceptions to standard policy (e.g., late checkout beyond Supervisor authority).
- Reviews and signs off on Manager-on-Duty log at end of shift.

### Housekeeping (Liaison Responsibilities)
- Communicates real-time room status updates to Front Desk via PMS.
- Prioritizes cleaning of rooms flagged for early check-in or VIP arrival.
- Reports maintenance issues discovered during room servicing.

### Security
- Responds to Front Desk calls regarding disturbances, unauthorized persons, or safety concerns.
- Assists with lost key investigations and access control issues.
- Coordinates with Duty Manager on incident documentation.

### Maintenance / Engineering
- Responds to guest room and public area repair requests logged by Front Desk.
- Prioritizes urgent repairs affecting room availability (e.g., HVAC, plumbing).

### Guest Relations
- Manages VIP arrival preparation in coordination with Front Desk.
- Handles proactive outreach to guests with special occasions or repeat-stay recognition.
- Supports Front Desk during high-volume complaint periods.

## 5. Standard Operating Procedure

### Phase 1: Pre-Arrival Preparation

**Purpose:** To ensure guest rooms, profiles, and special requests are fully prepared before guest arrival, minimizing delays and service errors at check-in.

**Required Materials:** PMS terminal, arrivals report, VIP list, room assignment sheet, guest profile notes.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Review the daily arrivals report at the start of each shift.
2. Cross-reference VIP list and flag all VIP arrivals for Guest Relations coordination.
3. Confirm room assignments align with guest preferences (e.g., high floor, king bed, accessible room).
4. Verify special requests (early check-in, late arrival, transportation) are noted as traces in the PMS.
5. Confirm room status ("Vacant Clean" or "Vacant Inspected") for all same-day arrivals by 12:00 PM.
6. Prepare key packets and welcome materials for VIP and loyalty-tier guests.

**Quality Checkpoints:**
- All VIP arrivals confirmed and communicated to Guest Relations no later than 10:00 AM.
- Room status discrepancies resolved before 2:00 PM standard check-in time.

**Expected Outcome:** All arriving guests have a fully prepared, verified reservation and room assignment prior to arrival, enabling a smooth and expedited check-in.

**Escalation Conditions:** If room status cannot be confirmed by 1:00 PM for a same-day arrival, escalate to Front Office Supervisor immediately.

---

### Phase 2: Guest Check-In

**Purpose:** To register arriving guests efficiently, accurately, and warmly in accordance with brand service standards.

**Required Materials:** PMS terminal, key card encoder, government-issued ID scanner, credit card terminal, welcome materials.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Greet the guest within 30 seconds of approaching the desk, using the guest's name if known.
2. Retrieve the reservation in the PMS using name, confirmation number, or phone number.
3. Verify guest identification against the reservation profile.
4. Confirm reservation details: dates, rate, room type, and any special requests.
5. Collect valid payment method and authorize an incidental hold in accordance with property policy.
6. Explain room location, applicable amenities, Wi-Fi access, and property services.
7. Issue encoded key cards, confirming the number of keys matches the number of registered guests.
8. Provide any pending messages, packages, or trace-related items.
9. Direct guest to elevators, Bell Staff, or room location as appropriate.
10. Update room status to "Occupied" in the PMS.

**Quality Checkpoints:**
- Check-in transaction completed within 5 minutes for standard guests; within 3 minutes for expedited/loyalty check-in.
- Guest name used a minimum of twice during the interaction.
- Payment authorization completed and confirmed before key issuance.

**Expected Outcome:** Guest is checked in accurately, feels welcomed, and proceeds to their room without delay or confusion.

**Escalation Conditions:** If the assigned room type is unavailable, room rate discrepancy exists, or the guest requests an unavailable upgrade, escalate to Front Office Supervisor before resolving independently.

---

### Phase 3: In-House Guest Servicing

**Purpose:** To support guests throughout their stay with accurate information, prompt service, and proactive issue resolution.

**Required Materials:** PMS terminal, telephone system, service request log, property directory.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Answer all incoming calls within three rings using the standard greeting script.
2. Log all guest requests (e.g., extra towels, maintenance issues, wake-up calls) into the PMS or service ticketing system.
3. Route requests to the appropriate department (Housekeeping, Maintenance, Room Service).
4. Follow up on service requests exceeding expected resolution time.
5. Document any guest complaints using the Guest Complaint Form (see Section 12).
6. Provide dining, transportation, and local area recommendations as requested.
7. Process room changes, folio adjustments, or billing questions as needed.

**Quality Checkpoints:**
- All service requests logged within 2 minutes of receipt.
- Follow-up on unresolved requests within 30 minutes.
- Guest complaints acknowledged verbally within 1 minute of being raised.

**Expected Outcome:** Guests receive timely, accurate assistance throughout their stay, and all requests are tracked to completion.

**Escalation Conditions:** Any complaint involving compensation, safety, or service failure beyond Agent authority must be escalated to the Supervisor or Duty Manager immediately.

---

### Phase 4: Guest Check-Out

**Purpose:** To ensure accurate, efficient, and courteous departure processing for all guests.

**Required Materials:** PMS terminal, printer, credit card terminal, folio records.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Greet the departing guest and retrieve their profile/folio in the PMS.
2. Review all charges with the guest, including room, incidentals, and applicable taxes.
3. Resolve any billing disputes prior to finalizing the folio.
4. Process final payment and close the folio.
5. Ask the guest about their overall stay experience.
6. Offer assistance with luggage, transportation, or future reservations.
7. Deactivate room keys in the PMS/key system.
8. Update room status to "Vacant Dirty" for Housekeeping.
9. Provide a printed or emailed receipt as requested.

**Quality Checkpoints:**
- Checkout transaction completed within 3 minutes for standard departures.
- All disputed charges resolved before folio closure.
- Room status updated within 2 minutes of key deactivation.

**Expected Outcome:** Guest departs with an accurate, clear final bill and a positive final impression of the property.

**Escalation Conditions:** Disputes exceeding Agent's authorized adjustment limit must be escalated to Supervisor or Duty Manager before resolution.

---

### Phase 5: Shift Handover

**Purpose:** To ensure continuity of operations and accurate information transfer between outgoing and incoming Front Desk shifts.

**Required Materials:** Shift handover log, cash drawer count sheet, PMS terminal.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Complete cash drawer reconciliation and document any variances.
2. Record all pending guest issues, traces, and unresolved requests in the handover log.
3. Brief incoming Agent verbally on VIPs, oversold risk, and open issues.
4. Confirm incoming Agent has reviewed the arrivals/departures report.
5. Both outgoing and incoming Agents sign the handover log.

**Quality Checkpoints:**
- Cash drawer variance does not exceed property-authorized threshold (typically $5.00).
- Handover log fully completed before outgoing Agent leaves the desk.

**Expected Outcome:** Incoming shift begins with full awareness of open issues, financial accuracy, and guest-specific needs.

**Escalation Conditions:** Cash discrepancies exceeding the authorized threshold must be reported to the Front Office Supervisor immediately and documented via Incident Report.

## 7. Best Practices

- **Use the guest's name naturally throughout the interaction.** This creates a personalized experience and reinforces brand hospitality standards, and is directly linked to higher guest satisfaction scores.
- **Confirm details before assuming.** Rate discrepancies and room type mismatches are among the top causes of guest complaints; verifying details upfront prevents downstream escalation.
- **Log everything in the PMS immediately.** Verbal-only requests are frequently lost during shift changes; documentation ensures continuity and accountability.
- **Anticipate needs during high-volume arrival periods.** Pre-preparing keys and packets for expected VIP or loyalty-tier arrivals reduces wait times and reflects positively on service metrics.
- **Maintain a calm, controlled demeanor during disputes.** Guests mirror staff tone; a calm Agent de-escalates faster than a defensive one.
- **Always offer a resolution, not just an apology.** Guests remember what was done to fix an issue far more than the apology itself.

## 8. Exceptions

**VIP Guests:** VIP arrivals receive expedited check-in, pre-assigned rooms, and personalized welcome amenities coordinated with Guest Relations. Standard check-in queue procedures may be bypassed with Supervisor awareness.

**Guests with Disabilities:** Accessible rooms must be held and not reassigned without guest consent. Staff must proactively confirm accessibility needs are met (grab bars, roll-in showers, visual/audio alert devices) prior to arrival.

**Late Arrivals (after 11:00 PM):** Guests with guaranteed reservations must have keys and paperwork pre-prepared by the overnight Agent to minimize wait time regardless of arrival hour.

**Early Check-In Requests:** Granted based on room availability and current occupancy; if unavailable, offer luggage storage and notify guest as soon as a room becomes available.

**Late Checkout / Extended Stay / Checkout Extension / Delayed Departure Requests:** May be granted at Agent discretion up to 1:00 PM based on occupancy forecast; requests beyond 1:00 PM require Supervisor approval due to Housekeeping turnover impact.

**System Failures (PMS Downtime):** Revert to manual check-in/check-out procedures using paper registration cards and manual folio tracking; reconcile all manual transactions into the PMS once systems are restored.

**Emergency Situations:** Front Desk defers to the Emergency Response Manual (ER-SOP-201) for evacuation, medical, or security emergencies; guest service transactions are suspended until the Duty Manager confirms it is safe to resume.

**Policy Exceptions:** Any deviation from standard policy (rate override, comp adjustment beyond Agent limit, guest relocation) requires Supervisor or Duty Manager approval and must be documented in the PMS notes.

## 9. Troubleshooting

**Problem: Guest's reservation cannot be located in the PMS.**
- *Possible Causes:* Booking made under a different name, third-party booking site delay, duplicate profile.
- *Resolution:* Search by confirmation number, phone number, or arrival date; check third-party booking portal directly if unresolved.
- *Escalation:* If unresolved within 5 minutes, notify Front Office Supervisor.

**Problem: Room is not ready at guest's arrival.**
- *Possible Causes:* Late checkout by previous guest, Housekeeping backlog, maintenance hold.
- *Resolution:* Offer luggage storage, complimentary beverage, and provide realistic time estimate; prioritize room in Housekeeping queue.
- *Escalation:* If delay exceeds 45 minutes, notify Duty Manager for possible compensation.

**Problem: Payment method is declined at check-in.**
- *Possible Causes:* Insufficient funds, incorrect card entry, bank hold flag.
- *Resolution:* Request alternate payment method; confirm card details were entered correctly.
- *Escalation:* If guest has no valid payment method, escalate to Supervisor for policy guidance.

**Problem: Guest disputes a charge on their folio.**
- *Possible Causes:* Miscommunication about rate, unauthorized charge, billing error.
- *Resolution:* Review folio line-by-line with guest; correct verified errors immediately.
- *Escalation:* Disputes beyond Agent's adjustment authority go to Supervisor or Duty Manager.

**Problem: Property is oversold for the night.**
- *Possible Causes:* Overbooking strategy, extended-stay guests not checking out as expected.
- *Resolution:* Identify walk candidates per property walk policy; secure comparable accommodation at a nearby property.
- *Escalation:* All walk decisions require Duty Manager approval.

**Problem: Guest reports a lost or malfunctioning room key.**
- *Possible Causes:* Demagnetized key, lost card, unauthorized access concern.
- *Resolution:* Verify guest identity before reissuing key; deactivate old key immediately.
- *Escalation:* If unauthorized access is suspected, notify Security immediately.

**Problem: PMS system is down.**
- *Possible Causes:* Network outage, server maintenance, software failure.
- *Resolution:* Switch to manual check-in/check-out procedures per Exceptions section.
- *Escalation:* Notify IT/Engineering and Duty Manager immediately.

## 10. Frequently Asked Questions

**1. What time is standard check-in?**
Standard check-in time is 3:00 PM. Rooms may be available earlier based on occupancy.

**2. What time is standard check-out?**
Standard check-out time is 11:00 AM. Late checkout, also called extended stay or checkout extension, may be granted per policy.

**3. Can I check a guest in early if their room is ready?**
Yes. If a room is in "Vacant Inspected" status and available, you may check the guest in early without requiring escalation.

**4. What do I do if a guest arrives without a reservation?**
Process them as a walk-in guest. Check current availability, quote the applicable rate, and follow standard check-in procedure.

**5. How much can I discount a rate without approval?**
Front Desk Agents may not apply rate discounts without Supervisor approval. All rate overrides must be logged with a reason code.

**6. What is the procedure if a guest's credit card is declined?**
Politely inform the guest and request an alternate payment method. Do not disclose the specific reason for decline, as this information is provided by the bank, not the hotel.

**7. How do I handle a guest who wants to extend their stay same-day?**
Check availability for the additional night(s) in the PMS. If available, extend the reservation and confirm updated rate and departure date with the guest.

**8. What should I do if a VIP guest arrives and their room is not ready?**
Notify the Duty Manager and Guest Relations immediately. Offer a complimentary amenity and provide priority room-readiness updates.

**9. Can I give out a guest's room number to another guest or visitor?**
No. Room numbers are confidential and must never be disclosed to third parties for guest safety and privacy.

**10. What is the correct way to greet a guest on the phone?**
Answer within three rings using the standard script: "Good [morning/afternoon/evening], Front Desk, this is [Name], how may I assist you?"

**11. What do I do if a guest complains about noise from another room?**
Apologize, document the complaint, and dispatch Security or a Manager to address the noise. Offer a room change if the issue persists.

**12. How do I process a checkout for a guest who already left without visiting the desk (express checkout)?**
Verify the folio is accurate, close it using the payment method on file, and email or mail the final receipt.

**13. What happens if a guest's key does not work?**
Verify their identification, re-encode a new key, and deactivate the old one. Apologize for the inconvenience.

**14. Can I move a guest to a different room after check-in?**
Yes, if necessary and the guest agrees. Update the PMS, re-key the new room, and deactivate the old room's keys.

**15. What is the policy on holding a room for a late arrival?**
Guaranteed reservations are held regardless of arrival time. Non-guaranteed reservations may be released at a property-specific cutoff time, typically 6:00 PM, unless otherwise noted.

**16. How do I handle a guest requesting a receipt after checkout?**
Retrieve the closed folio in the PMS using guest name or confirmation number and reissue the receipt via email or print.

**17. What should I do if I suspect a guest is under the influence and behaving erratically?**
Remain calm and courteous, avoid confrontation, and notify Security and the Duty Manager immediately.

**18. How do I handle a large group check-in?**
Coordinate in advance with the Group Reservations contact, pre-key rooms where possible, and consider setting up a dedicated check-in station for efficiency.

**19. What is the process for a guest who wants to dispute a charge from a previous stay?**
Direct the request to the Accounting or Guest Relations department, as Front Desk Agents typically cannot adjust closed folios from prior stays.

**20. What do I do if the fire alarm activates during check-in?**
Immediately stop the transaction, follow the Emergency Response Manual evacuation procedure, and assist guests toward the nearest exit.

**21. How should I respond if a guest asks for a discount because it's their birthday or anniversary?**
Acknowledge the occasion warmly and note it in their profile for recognition (e.g., amenity or note), but rate discounts still require Supervisor approval.

**22. What is the correct process for handling a "Do Not Disturb" request at the desk?**
Log the DND status in the PMS and inform relevant departments (Housekeeping) so the room is not entered until the guest removes the status.

## 13. Training Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Room Not Ready at Check-In
**Situation:** A guest with a confirmed reservation arrives at 3:15 PM, but their room is still showing "Vacant Dirty" in the PMS due to a Housekeeping backlog.

**Correct Response:** Apologize sincerely, offer complimentary luggage storage and a beverage, provide a realistic time estimate, and proactively update the guest once the room is ready. Notify Housekeeping to prioritize the room.

**Incorrect Response:** Telling the guest to "just wait" without explanation, providing no time estimate, and failing to follow up.

**Supervisor Notes:** Agents should always offer a concrete gesture (beverage, lounge access) and realistic communication rather than vague reassurance.

**Learning Outcome:** Proactive communication and small service gestures significantly reduce guest frustration during delays.

---

### Scenario 2: Guest Requests Late Checkout on a Sold-Out Night
**Situation:** A guest requests a 3:00 PM late checkout, but the hotel is fully sold out that night and Housekeeping needs maximum turnover time.

**Correct Response:** Explain the full-occupancy situation honestly, offer the latest checkout time operationally feasible (e.g., 1:00 PM), and offer luggage storage for after that time.

**Incorrect Response:** Approving the full request without checking occupancy, causing a same-day arrival's room to not be ready.

**Supervisor Notes:** Always check occupancy and Housekeeping capacity before approving extended stay requests beyond standard Agent authority.

**Learning Outcome:** Late checkout decisions must balance guest satisfaction against operational feasibility for incoming arrivals.

---

### Scenario 3: Suspected Unauthorized Person Requesting a Room Key
**Situation:** An individual approaches the desk claiming to be a guest's spouse and requests a duplicate key without being listed on the reservation.

**Correct Response:** Politely decline to issue a key, offer to call the registered guest to verify and authorize access, and never disclose room number or guest presence information.

**Incorrect Response:** Issuing a key based solely on the individual's claim without verification.

**Supervisor Notes:** Key control and guest privacy protocols must never be bypassed regardless of how credible the request seems.

**Learning Outcome:** Guest safety and privacy protocols take precedence over convenience in all key issuance situations.

---

### Scenario 4: Billing Dispute at Checkout
**Situation:** A guest disputes a $75 minibar charge, insisting they did not consume any items.

**Correct Response:** Review the folio and any Housekeeping/minibar attendant notes calmly with the guest; if unresolved, escalate to the Supervisor for a documented adjustment decision.

**Incorrect Response:** Immediately removing the charge without verification, or arguing with the guest about the charge's validity.

**Supervisor Notes:** Agents should verify before adjusting, but should never argue with a guest; escalation preserves both accuracy and guest relations.

**Learning Outcome:** Billing disputes require a balance of verification and empathetic communication, with clear escalation paths when resolution authority is exceeded.
