## 1. Purpose

This Customer Service Standards document (also referred to as "Guest Service Standards" or "Service Excellence Guidelines") establishes the property-wide behavioral, communication, and service recovery expectations that apply to every guest interaction, regardless of department. It defines the core service philosophy, communication standards, complaint handling procedures, and service recovery framework that ensure every guest receives consistent, attentive, and gracious service throughout their stay.

This document supplements department-specific SOPs by providing the universal service standard that underlies every guest touchpoint, and serves as an authoritative reference within the AI Operations Assistant knowledge base for guest service-related employee questions.

---

## 2. Scope

This document applies to every employee at the property who interacts with guests directly or indirectly, across all departments, including Front Desk, Housekeeping, Food & Beverage, Concierge, Spa, Valet, Security, and administrative staff who may encounter guests. It covers general service philosophy, communication and etiquette standards, complaint handling and service recovery, and guest feedback management. It does not replace department-specific operational procedures (e.g., check-in steps, room cleaning steps), which are addressed in their respective SOPs, but establishes the service tone and complaint-resolution framework that applies across all of them.

---

## 3. Definitions

- **Service Recovery**: The deliberate process of addressing and correcting a guest service failure to restore guest satisfaction and trust.
- **LEARN Model**: A structured complaint-handling framework — Listen, Empathize, Apologize, Resolve, Notify — used to guide staff through service recovery interactions.
- **Moment of Truth**: Any point of guest interaction that significantly shapes their overall perception of the property, whether positive or negative.
- **Guest Sentiment**: The overall emotional tone or satisfaction level expressed by a guest during an interaction or in feedback.
- **Empowerment Authority**: The pre-approved discretion given to employees to resolve guest issues on the spot, up to a defined limit, without requiring managerial approval.
- **Service Recovery Gesture**: A tangible or intangible gesture (e.g., complimentary item, rate adjustment, personalized note) offered to a guest following a service failure.
- **Net Promoter Score (NPS)**: A guest satisfaction metric measuring the likelihood of a guest recommending the property to others, commonly used to track service quality trends.
- **Guest Feedback Channel**: Any medium through which guest feedback is received, including in-person, post-stay surveys, online reviews, and social media.

## 4. Roles & Responsibilities

### All Employees
- Apply the property's core service standards (warmth, attentiveness, professionalism) in every guest interaction.
- Use the LEARN Model when addressing any guest complaint or service failure.
- Exercise Empowerment Authority within defined limits to resolve minor issues without unnecessary escalation.
- Report significant complaints or recurring service issues to their Supervisor.

### Department Supervisors
- Coach staff on service standards through observation and real-time feedback.
- Review and approve service recovery gestures exceeding Agent-level Empowerment Authority.
- Monitor guest feedback related to their department and address recurring patterns.

### Duty Manager
- Handles escalated guest complaints beyond Supervisor-level authority.
- Approves significant service recovery gestures (e.g., comped stays, substantial credits) within their authority level.
- Ensures consistent service recovery standards are applied across departments during their shift.

### Director of Guest Experience
- Monitors property-wide guest satisfaction metrics (NPS, review scores, survey data) and identifies improvement opportunities.
- Oversees service standards training curriculum and ongoing coaching programs.
- Reviews significant or recurring complaint patterns for root-cause process improvement.

### General Manager
- Sets the overall service culture and ensures department leadership models service excellence standards.
- Personally engages in service recovery for significant guest relations matters as appropriate.

## 5. Standard Operating Procedure

### Phase 1: Core Service Interaction Standards

**Purpose:** To ensure every guest interaction, regardless of department, reflects a consistent standard of warmth, attentiveness, and professionalism.

**Required Materials:** Service standards reference guide, name recognition practices.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Acknowledge guests promptly (within 30 seconds in person, within 3 rings by phone) with a warm, genuine greeting.
2. Use the guest's name naturally when known, without over-repetition.
3. Maintain attentive body language, eye contact, and a genuine, unhurried tone even during high-volume periods.
4. Anticipate guest needs proactively where possible, rather than waiting to be asked.
5. Close every interaction with a clear next step or warm closing statement, confirming the guest's needs have been met.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Guest greeting response time within 30 seconds in person; interactions reviewed periodically through guest feedback and Supervisor observation.

**Expected Outcome:** Guests experience consistent warmth and attentiveness across every department and touchpoint.

**Escalation Conditions:** Recurring feedback indicating a specific service gap is addressed through Supervisor coaching, and repeated issues are escalated to the Director of Guest Experience.

---

### Phase 2: Complaint Handling Using the LEARN Model

**Purpose:** To resolve guest complaints consistently and effectively using a structured, empathetic approach.

**Required Materials:** LEARN Model reference guide, Guest Complaint Form, Empowerment Authority guidelines.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. **Listen** — Give the guest full attention without interrupting, allowing them to fully explain the issue.
2. **Empathize** — Acknowledge the guest's frustration genuinely, validating their experience without becoming defensive.
3. **Apologize** — Offer a sincere, specific apology for the issue experienced, regardless of fault attribution at this stage.
4. **Resolve** — Take immediate action within your Empowerment Authority, or escalate promptly if the resolution exceeds your authority.
5. **Notify** — Inform the guest of the resolution taken and follow up to confirm their satisfaction; notify relevant departments of the issue for pattern tracking.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Complaint acknowledged within 1 minute; resolution offered or escalated within 5 minutes of full understanding of the issue.

**Expected Outcome:** Guest complaints are resolved calmly, empathetically, and effectively, often strengthening guest loyalty despite the initial issue.

**Escalation Conditions:** Any complaint exceeding Empowerment Authority, involving safety, or expressing significant guest dissatisfaction is escalated immediately to the Supervisor or Duty Manager.

---

### Phase 3: Service Recovery and Empowerment Authority

**Purpose:** To empower employees to resolve guest issues quickly and meaningfully without unnecessary delay or escalation.

**Required Materials:** Empowerment Authority guidelines, service recovery gesture options.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Assess the nature and severity of the service failure experienced by the guest.
2. Determine whether the appropriate resolution falls within your defined Empowerment Authority limit (e.g., complimentary item, minor credit, expedited service).
3. If within authority, resolve the issue directly and promptly without requiring Supervisor approval.
4. If beyond authority, escalate immediately to the Supervisor or Duty Manager with a clear summary of the issue and the guest's expectation.
5. Document the service recovery gesture provided for pattern tracking and departmental review.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Empowerment Authority applied consistently per defined limits; documentation completed for 100% of service recovery gestures.

**Expected Outcome:** Guests experience prompt, meaningful resolution without unnecessary delay, and the property maintains visibility into service recovery patterns.

**Escalation Conditions:** Any pattern of recurring service recovery needs in the same area is escalated to the Director of Guest Experience for root-cause review.

---

### Phase 4: Guest Feedback Management

**Purpose:** To systematically capture, respond to, and act upon guest feedback across all channels.

**Required Materials:** Post-stay survey system, online review monitoring tool, feedback tracking log.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Monitor all guest feedback channels (post-stay surveys, online reviews, social media, direct feedback) regularly.
2. Respond to online reviews and survey feedback professionally, genuinely, and within the property's defined response timeframe.
3. Route any specific service issue identified in feedback to the relevant department for follow-up or service recovery, including post-departure guests where appropriate.
4. Track feedback trends over time to identify recurring strengths or improvement areas.
5. Share relevant feedback trends with department leadership during regular service review meetings.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Online review responses provided within the property-defined timeframe (commonly 24–48 hours); feedback trends reviewed monthly.

**Expected Outcome:** Guest feedback is consistently captured, addressed, and used to drive ongoing service improvement.

**Escalation Conditions:** Any feedback indicating a significant safety, legal, or reputational concern is escalated immediately to the Director of Guest Experience and General Manager.

## 7. Best Practices

- **Apologize genuinely without over-explaining or making excuses.** A sincere, direct apology resolves guest frustration far more effectively than a lengthy justification of what went wrong.
- **Resolve issues at the first point of contact whenever possible.** Guests who must repeat their complaint to multiple staff members experience compounding frustration regardless of the eventual resolution quality.
- **Use Empowerment Authority proactively rather than defaulting to escalation.** Confident, immediate resolution within your authority builds guest trust faster than delayed managerial involvement for issues you are equipped to solve.
- **Treat every guest interaction as a potential Moment of Truth.** Guests often base their overall property perception on a small number of specific interactions, making consistency essential even in seemingly minor exchanges.
- **Respond to negative online feedback professionally and without defensiveness.** Prospective guests read how a property responds to criticism as closely as the original complaint itself.

## 8. Exceptions

**VIP Guests:** Service recovery for VIP guests is coordinated with Guest Relations per the VIP Guest Handling document (GR-SOP-401), though the same LEARN Model and sincerity standard applies to all guests regardless of tier.

**Guests with Disabilities:** Service standards apply equally, with additional attentiveness to ensuring communication and physical service delivery are fully accessible to the guest's specific needs.

**Group and Event Guests:** Complaints affecting an entire group or event are escalated to Sales and Catering leadership in addition to standard service recovery, given the broader scope of impact.

**Guests Expressing Extreme Dissatisfaction or Threatening Legal Action:** Escalate immediately to the Duty Manager and General Manager; frontline staff should not attempt to independently negotiate settlements or make legal representations.

**Social Media Public Complaints:** Do not engage in public back-and-forth debate; acknowledge publicly and professionally, then move the detailed resolution conversation to a private channel per property social media policy.

## 9. Troubleshooting

**Problem: A guest's complaint cannot be resolved within the employee's Empowerment Authority.**
- *Possible Causes:* Complaint severity exceeds standard resolution options, guest expectation exceeds standard policy.
- *Resolution:* Escalate promptly to the Supervisor or Duty Manager with a clear summary, ensuring the guest is not left waiting without communication.
- *Escalation:* Duty Manager for significant service recovery gestures beyond Supervisor authority.

**Problem: A guest remains dissatisfied despite a reasonable service recovery gesture.**
- *Possible Causes:* Underlying issue not fully understood, guest expectation misaligned with property policy, unrelated external frustration.
- *Resolution:* Revisit the LEARN Model, ensuring the guest feels genuinely heard; involve a Manager for a fresh perspective on the resolution offered.
- *Escalation:* Duty Manager or Director of Guest Experience for continued dissatisfaction.

**Problem: A negative online review contains factual inaccuracies about the stay.**
- *Possible Causes:* Guest misremembering details, genuine confusion, rare deliberate misrepresentation.
- *Resolution:* Respond professionally and factually without being confrontational, correcting any inaccuracy respectfully while still acknowledging the guest's overall experience.
- *Escalation:* Director of Guest Experience for reviews requiring a more significant or sensitive response.

**Problem: Multiple guests report the same service issue within a short period.**
- *Possible Causes:* A systemic operational issue (e.g., recurring maintenance failure, staffing gap) rather than isolated incidents.
- *Resolution:* Document the pattern and escalate to the relevant department Supervisor for root-cause investigation.
- *Escalation:* Director of Guest Experience for cross-departmental pattern review.

## 10. Frequently Asked Questions

**1. What is the LEARN Model, and when should I use it?**
Listen, Empathize, Apologize, Resolve, Notify — a structured framework used for handling any guest complaint or service recovery situation.

**2. How quickly should I acknowledge a guest complaint?**
Within 1 minute of the complaint being raised, even if full resolution takes longer.

**3. What is Empowerment Authority, and how do I know my limit?**
It is the pre-approved discretion to resolve guest issues on the spot up to a defined limit; confirm your specific department's limit with your Supervisor during training.

**4. Should I apologize even if the issue wasn't caused by my department?**
Yes. A sincere apology for the guest's experience is appropriate regardless of which department caused the original issue; internal fault attribution is handled separately from the guest-facing apology.

**5. What should I do if a guest's request falls outside standard policy?**
Assess whether it falls within your Empowerment Authority; if not, escalate promptly to your Supervisor rather than denying the request outright without review.

**6. How do I respond to a negative online review?**
Respond professionally and genuinely within the property's defined response timeframe, acknowledging the guest's experience and inviting further conversation through a private channel if needed.

**7. What if a guest is upset about something outside the property's control (e.g., weather, flight delays)?**
Still apply the LEARN Model with genuine empathy; while you cannot control external factors, guests appreciate acknowledgment and any reasonable accommodation within your authority.

**8. Can I offer a guest a free night or major credit on my own authority?**
Only if within your specific Empowerment Authority limit; significant gestures typically require Supervisor or Duty Manager approval.

**9. What should I do if I overhear a guest expressing frustration but they haven't complained to me directly?**
Proactively and politely offer assistance rather than waiting for a formal complaint, as early intervention often prevents escalation.

**10. How do I handle a guest complaint about another guest's behavior (e.g., noise)?**
Apologize for the disruption, address the situation appropriately (e.g., contacting Security or the other room), and follow up with the complaining guest to confirm resolution.

**11. What is a "Moment of Truth"?**
Any guest interaction point that significantly shapes their overall perception of the property, whether the moment is handled well or poorly.

**12. Do I need to document every service recovery gesture I provide?**
Yes. Documentation of service recovery gestures supports pattern tracking and departmental review, regardless of gesture size.

**13. What should I do if a guest complaint involves a legal threat?**
Escalate immediately to the Duty Manager and General Manager; do not attempt to independently negotiate a settlement or make legal representations.

**14. How should I handle a public complaint posted on social media?**
Acknowledge it publicly and professionally without debating details, then move the detailed resolution conversation to a private channel.

**15. What if I genuinely don't know how to resolve a guest's issue?**
It is appropriate to tell the guest you want to ensure they get the best possible resolution and involve your Supervisor, rather than guessing or providing inaccurate information.

**16. How often is guest feedback reviewed at the department level?**
Feedback trends are reviewed monthly, though significant individual issues are addressed as they arise.

**17. What is Net Promoter Score (NPS), and why does it matter to my role?**
It is a guest satisfaction metric reflecting how likely guests are to recommend the property; consistent service quality across every role directly influences this score.

**18. Should service standards be different for VIP guests versus standard guests?**
The core sincerity and LEARN Model standard applies equally to all guests; VIP guests receive additional coordination through Guest Relations, not a different service philosophy.

**19. What if a guest's complaint seems unreasonable or exaggerated?**
Still apply the LEARN Model with genuine empathy and professionalism; dismissing a guest's perception, even if it seems exaggerated, typically escalates rather than resolves the situation.

**20. Who do I notify after resolving a guest complaint?**
Notify your Supervisor and relevant department per the LEARN Model's final step, supporting pattern tracking and ensuring the guest's satisfaction is confirmed.

## 13. Training Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Guest Complains About a Delayed Room Service Order
**Situation:** A guest calls the Front Desk frustrated that their room service order is 45 minutes late.

**Correct Response:** The Agent listens fully, apologizes sincerely for the delay, checks on the order status immediately, offers an appropriate service recovery gesture within their Empowerment Authority (e.g., complimentary item), and follows up once the order arrives to confirm satisfaction.

**Incorrect Response:** Explaining that the kitchen is busy without acknowledging the guest's frustration or offering any resolution.

**Supervisor Notes:** Explaining the cause of a delay is secondary to acknowledging the guest's experience and taking ownership of a resolution.

**Learning Outcome:** The LEARN Model applies regardless of which department caused the original issue; the guest-facing employee owns the resolution experience.

---

### Scenario 2: Guest Leaves a Negative Online Review After Checkout
**Situation:** A guest posts a review mentioning a housekeeping oversight and a slow check-in experience during their stay.

**Correct Response:** The Director of Guest Experience or designated Manager responds professionally and specifically within the defined timeframe, acknowledging both issues, and reaches out privately if contact information is available to offer a genuine resolution.

**Incorrect Response:** Leaving the review unanswered, or posting a generic, impersonal response that doesn't address the specific issues raised.

**Supervisor Notes:** Specific, genuine responses to online feedback demonstrate accountability to both the reviewing guest and prospective future guests reading the review.

**Learning Outcome:** Online feedback response quality directly reflects the property's service culture to a much wider audience than the original guest alone.

---

### Scenario 3: Guest Complaint Exceeds Employee's Empowerment Authority
**Situation:** A guest requests a significant compensation (e.g., a comped night) following a service failure that exceeds a Front Desk Agent's standard authority limit.

**Correct Response:** The Agent listens, empathizes, and apologizes genuinely, then explains they want to ensure the guest receives the best possible resolution and promptly involves the Duty Manager rather than denying or independently approving the request.

**Incorrect Response:** Denying the request outright due to lack of authority, or promising a resolution the Agent isn't authorized to guarantee.

**Supervisor Notes:** Prompt, transparent escalation preserves guest trust far better than either an unauthorized promise or an unexplained denial.

**Learning Outcome:** Recognizing the boundary of one's Empowerment Authority and escalating smoothly is itself a core service skill, not a failure to resolve independently.
