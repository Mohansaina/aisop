## 1. Purpose

This Housekeeping Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes consistent, measurable processes for cleaning, inspecting, and maintaining guest rooms and public areas to the property's luxury service standards. It governs room servicing (also referred to as "room cleaning," "room turnover," or "room attendant servicing"), departure cleaning, stayover servicing, deep cleaning, public area maintenance, linen and amenity management, and lost property handling.

This document ensures Housekeeping Attendants, Supervisors, and Managers deliver a consistent guest experience while supporting accurate, real-time room status communication to Front Desk and other departments. It also serves as an authoritative reference within the AI Operations Assistant knowledge base for Housekeeping-related employee questions.

---

## 2. Scope

This SOP applies to all Housekeeping personnel, including:

- Room Attendants (also referred to as "Housekeepers" or "Room Care Specialists")
- Housekeeping Supervisors
- Housekeeping Floor Inspectors
- Executive Housekeeper / Director of Housekeeping
- Public Area Attendants
- Laundry/Linen Room staff

This SOP covers guest room servicing, public area cleaning, linen and amenity inventory, lost and found intake, and coordination with Front Desk and Maintenance. It does not cover Front Desk check-in/check-out transactions, Food & Beverage service standards, or Engineering repair execution, which are addressed in separate departmental SOPs.

## 3. Definitions

- **Room Turnover (also called "Room Servicing," "Room Cleaning," or "Room Attendant Servicing")**: The complete process of cleaning and resetting a guest room following departure or during a stay.
- **Departure Clean**: A full room clean performed after a guest has checked out and vacated the room.
- **Stayover Clean**: A lighter servicing performed while a guest remains in-house for multiple nights, typically including bed making, trash removal, and bathroom refresh.
- **Deep Clean**: An intensive, periodic cleaning beyond standard servicing, covering areas such as carpets, upholstery, and behind-furniture spaces.
- **Room Status Codes**: PMS indicators reflecting room readiness — "Vacant Dirty," "Vacant Clean," "Vacant Inspected," and "Occupied."
- **Par Level**: The standard quantity of linens, amenities, or supplies that must be maintained on a housekeeping cart or in a storage area at all times.
- **Turndown Service**: An evening service that resets the room for nighttime use, typically including bed turndown, curtain closure, and amenity replenishment.
- **Do Not Disturb (DND)**: A guest-activated status indicating the room should not be entered by staff.
- **Inspection**: The Supervisor or Inspector's quality review of a room after cleaning, confirming it meets brand standards before being marked "Vacant Inspected."
- **Lost and Found (also called "Lost Property")**: The process and storage system for items left behind by guests and recovered by Housekeeping or other departments.
- **OOO (Out of Order)**: A room status indicating the room is not sellable due to maintenance, damage, or renovation.
- **OOS (Out of Service)**: A room status indicating a temporary sellability restriction, often shorter-term than OOO.

## 4. Roles & Responsibilities

### Room Attendant
- Cleans and services assigned guest rooms per shift assignment sheet.
- Restocks linens, amenities, and minibar items according to par levels.
- Reports maintenance issues discovered during servicing.
- Updates room status upon completion of cleaning.
- Follows lost property intake procedure for any items found.

### Housekeeping Supervisor
- Assigns daily room boards to Room Attendants.
- Conducts quality inspections on a sample or full basis per property policy.
- Coordinates priority cleaning for VIP arrivals, early check-ins, and same-day requests.
- Manages linen and amenity inventory levels.
- Escalates unresolved maintenance or guest issues to the Executive Housekeeper.

### Floor Inspector
- Conducts room inspections following cleaning, verifying compliance with quality checkpoints.
- Marks rooms "Vacant Inspected" in the PMS upon passing inspection.
- Documents any deficiencies requiring re-cleaning.

### Executive Housekeeper / Director of Housekeeping
- Oversees departmental staffing, scheduling, and training.
- Reviews inspection compliance rates and quality audit results.
- Manages departmental budget for supplies, linens, and equipment.
- Coordinates with Front Office on room status accuracy and turnover priorities.

### Front Desk (Liaison Responsibilities)
- Communicates arrivals requiring priority cleaning (VIP, early check-in) to Housekeeping.
- Relays guest-reported room issues discovered post-check-in to Housekeeping.

### Maintenance / Engineering (Liaison Responsibilities)
- Responds to maintenance issues flagged by Housekeeping during room servicing.
- Coordinates room OOO/OOS status changes with Housekeeping and Front Desk.

## 5. Standard Operating Procedure

### Phase 1: Daily Room Board Assignment

**Purpose:** To distribute room servicing workload efficiently and ensure priority rooms are serviced first.

**Required Materials:** PMS room status report, daily arrivals/departures report, room assignment sheets.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Supervisor reviews the departures report and arrivals report at shift start.
2. Rooms are assigned to Room Attendants based on standard workload allocation (typically 12–16 rooms per 8-hour shift).
3. VIP arrivals, early check-in requests, and accessible room requests are flagged as priority.
4. Room assignment sheets are distributed to each Attendant with priority order indicated.
5. Attendants confirm receipt of cart supplies and linen par levels before beginning.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Priority rooms identified and assigned before 9:00 AM daily.

**Expected Outcome:** All Attendants begin their shift with a clear, prioritized room assignment list.

**Escalation Conditions:** If staffing shortages prevent full room board coverage, escalate to Executive Housekeeper immediately for reassignment or overtime authorization.

---

### Phase 2: Departure Room Cleaning

**Purpose:** To fully reset a vacated room to brand cleanliness and presentation standards for the next guest.

**Required Materials:** Housekeeping cart, cleaning chemicals, linens, amenities, vacuum, PMS-connected device or phone system for status updates.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Confirm room is marked "Vacant Dirty" before entry; knock and announce presence before entering.
2. Open curtains and windows (where applicable) to air the room.
3. Remove all trash, used linens, and room service items.
4. Strip and remake the bed with fresh linens according to brand bed-making standard.
5. Clean and sanitize the bathroom completely, including tub/shower, toilet, sink, and mirrors.
6. Dust and wipe all surfaces, including furniture, electronics, and fixtures.
7. Vacuum or clean flooring throughout the room.
8. Restock amenities, minibar, and collateral materials to par level.
9. Perform a final visual walkthrough to confirm presentation standards are met.
10. Update room status to "Vacant Clean" in the PMS or via handheld device.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Departure clean completed within 30 minutes average per standard room.

**Expected Outcome:** Room fully reset to brand standard, ready for inspection.

**Escalation Conditions:** If maintenance issues are discovered (e.g., broken fixture, HVAC malfunction), report to Supervisor and Maintenance immediately before marking room ready.

---

### Phase 3: Stayover Room Servicing

**Purpose:** To maintain cleanliness and guest comfort for in-house guests without full departure-level reset.

**Required Materials:** Housekeeping cart, cleaning chemicals, fresh linens and towels.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Confirm room is not marked "Do Not Disturb" before entry; knock and announce presence.
2. Remove trash and used room service items.
3. Make the bed, replacing linens per property linen-change policy (typically every 3 days or upon guest request, consistent with sustainability programs).
4. Replace used towels and replenish bathroom amenities.
5. Wipe down bathroom surfaces and re-sanitize as needed.
6. Vacuum or spot-clean flooring.
7. Straighten guest belongings neatly without relocating personal items unnecessarily.
8. Update room status upon completion.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Stayover service completed within 20 minutes average per room.

**Expected Outcome:** Room remains clean, comfortable, and well-stocked throughout the guest's stay.

**Escalation Conditions:** If a DND status remains active past a property-defined threshold (typically 2:00 PM), notify Supervisor for guest welfare check coordination with Front Desk.

---

### Phase 4: Inspection

**Purpose:** To verify that cleaned rooms meet brand quality standards before being marked available for sale.

**Required Materials:** Inspection checklist, PMS-connected device.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Inspector reviews room using the standardized Inspection Form (see Section 12).
2. Checks bed presentation, bathroom cleanliness, dusting, flooring, amenity stocking, and overall presentation.
3. Documents any deficiencies requiring correction.
4. If deficiencies are found, room is returned to the Attendant for correction before re-inspection.
5. Upon passing inspection, room status is updated to "Vacant Inspected" in the PMS.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Minimum inspection rate per property policy (commonly 100% of departure rooms, or a defined sample percentage for stayover rooms).

**Expected Outcome:** Only fully compliant rooms are marked available for guest occupancy.

**Escalation Conditions:** Repeated deficiencies by the same Attendant are reported to the Supervisor for coaching or retraining.

---

### Phase 5: Priority and Rush Cleaning

**Purpose:** To expedite room readiness for VIP arrivals, early check-ins, or same-day urgent requests.

**Required Materials:** Same as standard departure cleaning; priority flag in PMS or assignment sheet.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Supervisor identifies priority rooms from the arrivals report or Front Desk request.
2. Reassign an Attendant or team to the priority room ahead of standard board order.
3. Complete full departure clean per Phase 2 standards, without shortcuts to quality checkpoints.
4. Immediately notify Front Desk upon completion and inspection pass.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Priority rooms completed and inspected within 45 minutes of request where operationally feasible.

**Expected Outcome:** VIP and priority guests experience minimal wait time without any reduction in room quality.

**Escalation Conditions:** If priority room cannot be completed within the expected window, notify Front Desk and Executive Housekeeper immediately with a revised time estimate.

## 7. Best Practices

- **Always knock and announce before entry, regardless of DND status assumptions.** This protects guest privacy and safety and is a non-negotiable standard across all room types.
- **Report maintenance issues immediately rather than working around them.** A small unreported issue (e.g., a loose fixture) can become a larger guest complaint or safety concern if left unresolved.
- **Maintain consistent bed-making and presentation standards across all rooms.** Consistency is a core driver of guest perception of quality and brand trust.
- **Prioritize VIP and early check-in rooms without compromising quality checkpoints.** Speed should never come at the expense of the same inspection standard applied to all rooms.
- **Communicate room status changes in real time.** Delayed status updates directly impact Front Desk's ability to check in arriving guests efficiently.

## 8. Exceptions

**VIP Guest Rooms:** Require priority servicing and may include enhanced amenity placement coordinated with Guest Relations; inspection standards remain identical to standard rooms.

**Guests with Disabilities:** Accessible room features (grab bars, roll-in showers, visual alert devices) must be checked for functionality during every servicing, not only at initial setup.

**Do Not Disturb Rooms:** Must not be entered under any circumstance without Supervisor and Duty Manager coordination for a documented welfare check if DND persists beyond the property-defined threshold.

**Late Checkout / Extended Stay Rooms:** Serviced after guest departure per standard turnaround; Housekeeping should be notified in advance by Front Desk to adjust attendant scheduling.

**System Failures (PMS Downtime):** Room status updates are tracked manually via paper room board and communicated to Front Desk by phone until systems are restored.

**Emergency Situations:** Housekeeping defers to the Emergency Response Manual (ER-SOP-201) for evacuation or safety emergencies; all servicing activity is suspended until cleared by the Duty Manager.

## 9. Troubleshooting

**Problem: Attendant discovers guest belongings during a departure clean, and it is unclear if the guest has fully checked out.**
- *Possible Causes:* Guest departed without fully packing, early departure error in PMS.
- *Resolution:* Do not discard any items; notify Supervisor and Front Desk immediately to confirm checkout status before proceeding.
- *Escalation:* If checkout status cannot be confirmed, escalate to Duty Manager.

**Problem: Room fails inspection multiple times.**
- *Possible Causes:* Attendant training gap, inadequate time allocation, missing supplies.
- *Resolution:* Supervisor re-cleans or coaches on-site; review Attendant's workload and training needs.
- *Escalation:* Repeated failures reported to Executive Housekeeper for formal retraining.

**Problem: Insufficient linens or amenities to complete room board.**
- *Possible Causes:* Inventory shortage, delayed laundry return, ordering delay.
- *Resolution:* Notify Supervisor immediately to redistribute available stock by priority; expedite laundry turnaround if applicable.
- *Escalation:* Persistent shortages escalated to Executive Housekeeper for supply chain review.

**Problem: Maintenance issue discovered prevents room from being marked ready.**
- *Possible Causes:* Plumbing, HVAC, electrical, or structural issue.
- *Resolution:* Submit Maintenance Request Form immediately; mark room OOO/OOS in PMS until resolved.
- *Escalation:* Urgent issues affecting same-day availability are escalated to Executive Housekeeper and Front Office Manager jointly.

**Problem: Guest complains that stayover room was not serviced.**
- *Possible Causes:* DND sign left up inadvertently, room missed on board, guest request for no service not logged.
- *Resolution:* Apologize, verify room board assignment, and dispatch immediate servicing if guest confirms consent.
- *Escalation:* Recurring missed rooms reported to Supervisor for root-cause review.

## 10. Frequently Asked Questions

**1. How many rooms is a Room Attendant expected to clean per shift?**
Standard quota is 12–16 rooms per 8-hour shift, adjusted for room type and property-specific standards.

**2. What is the difference between a departure clean and a stayover clean?**
A departure clean is a full reset performed after a guest checks out, while a stayover clean is a lighter servicing performed for guests remaining in-house.

**3. How often should linens be changed for stayover guests?**
Every 3 days or upon guest request, consistent with property sustainability programs, unless the guest requests daily changes.

**4. What should I do if I find a "Do Not Disturb" sign on a stayover room?**
Do not enter. Note it on the room board and inform your Supervisor if the DND status persists past the property-defined threshold.

**5. What do I do if I find personal items left behind in a departed guest's room?**
Log the item immediately using the Lost Property Form and turn it in to the designated Lost and Found storage area.

**6. Who inspects rooms after cleaning?**
A Floor Inspector or Housekeeping Supervisor inspects rooms and marks them "Vacant Inspected" upon passing.

**7. What happens if a room fails inspection?**
It is returned to the Attendant for correction and re-inspected before being marked ready for guest occupancy.

**8. How do I report a maintenance issue found while cleaning?**
Submit a Maintenance Request Form immediately and notify your Supervisor; do not mark the room as ready until the issue is addressed or reviewed.

**9. What is the process for priority cleaning a VIP room?**
The Supervisor reassigns the room ahead of standard board order and ensures it is completed and inspected within the priority timeframe, without reducing quality checkpoints.

**10. Can I skip restocking amenities if the guest didn't use them?**
No. All amenities must be restocked to par level regardless of prior usage, unless the property has an opt-out sustainability program in place.

**11. What do I do if a guest is present in the room during a scheduled stayover clean?**
Politely ask if it is a convenient time to service the room, or offer to return at a preferred time.

**12. How should I handle a room marked "Vacant Dirty" that appears to already be clean?**
Verify with the Supervisor before updating status; do not assume the PMS status is incorrect without confirmation.

**13. What is the correct procedure if I accidentally enter an occupied room without knocking?**
Apologize immediately to the guest, exit, and notify your Supervisor of the incident for documentation.

**14. How long should a standard departure clean take?**
Approximately 30 minutes per standard room, though this may vary based on room size and condition.

**15. What should I do if I run out of a specific amenity type mid-shift?**
Notify your Supervisor immediately to redistribute available stock or expedite restocking.

**16. Are accessible rooms cleaned differently than standard rooms?**
The general cleaning process is the same, but Attendants must verify all accessibility features are present and functioning during every servicing.

**17. What is the procedure for a room marked Out of Order (OOO)?**
Do not service the room for guest readiness; only enter if instructed for maintenance-related cleaning support.

**18. How do I know which rooms need priority servicing on a given day?**
The Supervisor flags VIP, early check-in, and urgent requests on the daily room assignment sheet each morning.

**19. What should I do if a guest requests a change to their turndown service preference?**
Log the preference in the guest profile or trace and notify your Supervisor so it is reflected in future servicing.

**20. What is the process if I discover damage in a room that appears to be beyond normal wear?**
Document the damage, notify your Supervisor immediately, and do not disturb the area until it has been reviewed for potential guest billing or incident reporting.

## 13. Training Scenarios

### Scenario 1: DND Sign Present for an Extended Period
**Situation:** A room has displayed a "Do Not Disturb" sign for over 24 hours, and the Attendant is unsure whether to proceed with servicing.

**Correct Response:** Do not enter the room. Report the extended DND status to the Supervisor, who coordinates with Front Desk and the Duty Manager for a guest welfare check if needed.

**Incorrect Response:** Entering the room without authorization because "it's been too long."

**Supervisor Notes:** DND status must always be respected; welfare checks are handled through a documented, cross-departmental process, not by Housekeeping alone.

**Learning Outcome:** Guest privacy protocols remain absolute, and any exception requires proper escalation, not independent Attendant judgment.

---

### Scenario 2: Guest Belongings Found in a Room Marked as Departed
**Situation:** During a departure clean, the Attendant finds personal belongings still in the closet, and the room shows as checked out in the PMS.

**Correct Response:** Do not discard or move the items carelessly. Log them per the Lost Property Form and notify the Supervisor and Front Desk immediately to confirm checkout accuracy.

**Incorrect Response:** Assuming the items are trash and discarding them, or continuing the clean without reporting the find.

**Supervisor Notes:** All discovered items must be treated as guest property until confirmed otherwise; discarding items without verification creates liability risk.

**Learning Outcome:** Lost property procedures protect both the guest and the property and must be followed precisely regardless of assumed circumstances.

---

### Scenario 3: Room Fails Inspection Repeatedly
**Situation:** A specific Room Attendant's rooms have failed inspection three times in one week for inconsistent bed-making and dusting.

**Correct Response:** Supervisor provides on-site coaching, reviews time allocation and technique, and documents the pattern for the Executive Housekeeper.

**Incorrect Response:** Ignoring the pattern and allowing rooms to be marked ready despite repeated deficiencies.

**Supervisor Notes:** Consistent quality issues require structured coaching and documentation rather than one-off corrections.

**Learning Outcome:** Inspection standards must be enforced consistently, and recurring issues call for proactive training intervention.
