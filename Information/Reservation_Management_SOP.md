## 1. Purpose

This Reservation Management Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the processes governing the creation, modification, confirmation, and cancellation of guest reservations across all booking channels, including direct phone bookings, the property website, third-party online travel agencies (OTAs), global distribution systems (GDS), and group/corporate booking contracts. It ensures rate accuracy, inventory control, and consistent guest communication throughout the reservation lifecycle.

This document also defines overbooking management, rate code governance, and cancellation/no-show policy administration, and serves as an authoritative reference within the AI Operations Assistant knowledge base for reservation-related employee questions.

---

## 2. Scope

This SOP applies to all employees involved in reservation creation, modification, or management, including:

- Reservations Agents
- Reservations Supervisors / Manager
- Director of Reservations / Revenue Management
- Sales and Catering staff creating group block reservations
- Front Desk Agents modifying reservations at or near arrival

It covers individual reservation booking, rate code application, group block management, overbooking/oversold inventory control, cancellation and no-show policy administration, and reservation confirmation communication. It does not cover the physical check-in/check-out transaction process (see FO-SOP-102) or Front Desk day-of-arrival room assignment, which are addressed in separate departmental SOPs.

## 3. Definitions

- **Rate Code**: An alphanumeric code within the PMS identifying the pricing category and terms applied to a reservation (e.g., corporate rate, promotional rate, loyalty rate).
- **Rate Parity**: The practice of maintaining consistent pricing for the same room type and dates across all distribution channels.
- **GDS (Global Distribution System)**: The electronic platform used by travel agents and corporate booking tools to search and book hotel inventory.
- **OTA (Online Travel Agency)**: A third-party website or platform (e.g., booking aggregators) through which guests can reserve rooms, typically subject to a commission agreement with the property.
- **Inventory Control**: The management of room availability across all distribution channels to prevent overselling or underselling of room types.
- **Overbooking (also called "Oversold Situation")**: A condition where confirmed reservations exceed available room inventory for a given night, typically managed intentionally within a controlled percentage to account for expected no-shows and cancellations.
- **Guarantee Policy**: The payment or credit card requirement securing a reservation against late cancellation or no-show.
- **No-Show**: A guest who fails to arrive for a confirmed reservation without prior cancellation, typically resulting in a no-show charge per the guarantee policy.
- **Group Block**: A set of rooms reserved under a single contract for a group, event, or corporate account, typically with a specific rate, cutoff date, and attrition terms.
- **Cutoff Date**: The date by which unbooked rooms within a group block are released back into general inventory.
- **Attrition**: The percentage of contracted group rooms that must be picked up (booked) to avoid financial penalty per the group contract.
- **Waitlist**: A list of guests interested in a reservation for dates currently at full inventory, contacted if availability opens due to cancellation.

## 4. Roles & Responsibilities

### Reservations Agent
- Processes individual reservation requests via phone, email, or direct booking channels.
- Applies correct rate codes and verifies rate accuracy at time of booking.
- Explains cancellation, guarantee, and no-show policies clearly to guests at time of booking.
- Processes reservation modifications and cancellations per policy.

### Reservations Supervisor / Manager
- Oversees daily inventory control and monitors sold-out or high-demand date ranges.
- Manages overbooking strategy within approved property guidelines.
- Resolves rate discrepancies and reservation disputes escalated by Agents.
- Coordinates with Revenue Management on rate strategy execution.

### Director of Reservations / Revenue Management
- Sets rate strategy, inventory allocation, and overbooking thresholds in coordination with the General Manager.
- Monitors channel performance (direct, OTA, GDS) and rate parity compliance.
- Approves group block contracts and attrition/cutoff terms in coordination with Sales.

### Sales and Catering
- Negotiates and creates group block reservations for events, conferences, and corporate accounts.
- Monitors group block pickup against cutoff dates and communicates attrition risk to Reservations and Revenue Management.

### Front Desk (Liaison Responsibilities)
- Modifies reservations for arriving or in-house guests as needed (e.g., extending a stay).
- Flags any rate discrepancies discovered at check-in to Reservations for correction and review.

## 5. Standard Operating Procedure

### Phase 1: Individual Reservation Creation

**Purpose:** To accurately capture and confirm a guest's reservation request across any booking channel.

**Required Materials:** PMS terminal, rate code reference guide, guarantee/cancellation policy reference.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Gather guest information: name, contact details, arrival/departure dates, room type preference, and number of guests.
2. Check availability for the requested dates and room type in the PMS.
3. Apply the correct rate code based on the guest's eligibility (e.g., public rate, corporate rate, loyalty rate, promotional rate).
4. Clearly explain the guarantee policy, cancellation policy, and any deposit requirement to the guest before finalizing.
5. Collect guarantee payment information (credit card) per property policy.
6. Confirm all reservation details verbally with the guest before completing the booking.
7. Provide a confirmation number and send written confirmation (email) to the guest.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Reservation details confirmed verbally with guest in 100% of phone bookings; written confirmation sent within 5 minutes of booking completion.

**Expected Outcome:** An accurate, fully documented reservation with clear guest understanding of applicable policies.

**Escalation Conditions:** If the requested rate cannot be applied due to eligibility restrictions, offer the appropriate available rate and escalate to Supervisor if the guest disputes eligibility.

---

### Phase 2: Rate Code Governance and Inventory Control

**Purpose:** To maintain rate accuracy, parity, and appropriate inventory allocation across all distribution channels.

**Required Materials:** PMS/Channel Manager, rate parity monitoring tool, inventory allocation report.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Revenue Management reviews and sets rate strategy by date, room type, and channel based on demand forecasting.
2. Confirm rate parity compliance across direct, OTA, and GDS channels regularly.
3. Adjust inventory allocation across channels based on real-time demand and booking pace.
4. Monitor and correct any rate loading errors identified through Agent reports or automated parity monitoring.
5. Communicate any rate strategy changes to Reservations Agents and Front Desk promptly.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Rate parity discrepancies identified and corrected within 24 hours of detection.

**Expected Outcome:** Consistent, accurate rate presentation across all booking channels supporting guest trust and revenue optimization.

**Escalation Conditions:** Significant or recurring parity violations from a specific OTA partner are escalated to the Director of Reservations for partner-level resolution.

---

### Phase 3: Overbooking and Oversold Management

**Purpose:** To manage controlled overbooking within approved limits while minimizing guest impact from oversold situations.

**Required Materials:** Overbooking strategy guidelines, no-show/cancellation forecast data, walk policy reference.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Revenue Management sets an approved overbooking percentage by date based on historical no-show and cancellation patterns.
2. Reservations Supervisor monitors booking pace daily against the overbooking threshold for upcoming dates.
3. If a date approaches a true oversold risk (confirmed reservations likely to exceed physical inventory), notify the Duty Manager and Front Office in advance.
4. On the day of a confirmed oversold situation, Front Office and Duty Manager identify walk candidates per the property's walk policy (see Front Desk Operations SOP, FO-SOP-101).
5. Document all walked guests and the resolution provided for post-incident review.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Oversold situations identified and communicated to Front Office at least 24 hours in advance where possible.

**Expected Outcome:** Overbooking is managed strategically to maximize occupancy while minimizing last-minute guest displacement.

**Escalation Conditions:** Any oversold situation without adequate advance notice is escalated to the Director of Reservations for process review.

---

### Phase 4: Group Block Management

**Purpose:** To accurately manage group reservation blocks from contract signing through cutoff and final pickup.

**Required Materials:** Group contract, block rooming list, cutoff date tracking report.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Sales and Catering finalizes the group contract, including room block size, rate, cutoff date, and attrition terms.
2. Reservations loads the group block into the PMS with the appropriate rate code and room type allocation.
3. Monitor pickup pace against the block leading up to the cutoff date.
4. Notify Sales and Revenue Management if pickup is trending toward attrition risk ahead of the cutoff date.
5. Release unbooked rooms back into general inventory at the cutoff date per contract terms, unless an extension is approved.
6. Process individual rooming list reservations within the block per standard reservation procedures.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Cutoff date monitoring and attrition risk communication completed at least 2 weeks before cutoff where contract terms allow.

**Expected Outcome:** Group blocks are accurately managed with rooms appropriately allocated, tracked, and released per contract terms.

**Escalation Conditions:** Any group contract dispute regarding attrition or cutoff terms is escalated to the Director of Reservations and Sales leadership.

---

### Phase 5: Modification, Cancellation, and No-Show Processing

**Purpose:** To process guest-requested changes and enforce guarantee policy consistently and fairly.

**Required Materials:** PMS terminal, cancellation/no-show policy reference, guarantee payment records.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Verify the guest's identity and reservation details before processing any modification or cancellation.
2. For cancellations, confirm whether the request falls within the penalty-free cancellation window per the guarantee policy.
3. Process the cancellation and provide a cancellation confirmation number to the guest.
4. For no-shows, apply the no-show charge per policy on the night following the missed arrival date, unless the reservation was flexible/unguaranteed.
5. Document any cancellation or no-show dispute and escalate per policy if the guest contests the charge.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Cancellation confirmation provided to guest in 100% of processed cancellations; no-show charges applied consistently per policy.

**Expected Outcome:** Reservation changes are processed accurately, and guarantee policy is enforced fairly and consistently.

**Escalation Conditions:** Disputed no-show or cancellation charges beyond Agent authority are escalated to the Reservations Supervisor or Duty Manager.

## 7. Best Practices

- **Always confirm the guarantee and cancellation policy clearly at time of booking, not after.** Guests who understand the policy upfront are far less likely to dispute charges later, and clear disclosure protects the property from chargebacks.
- **Monitor rate parity proactively rather than reactively.** Catching a parity issue before a guest notices a discrepancy protects both guest trust and channel partner relationships.
- **Communicate oversold risk to Front Office as early as possible.** Early notice allows more options for proactive resolution (e.g., soliciting voluntary relocation) rather than last-minute walks.
- **Track group block pickup continuously, not just near the cutoff date.** Early trend identification allows Sales more time to drive pickup or renegotiate terms before attrition penalties become unavoidable.
- **Document every guest interaction involving a policy explanation or dispute.** This protects both the guest and the property in the event of a later billing question or chargeback dispute.

## 8. Exceptions

**Loyalty Program Elite Members:** May be eligible for flexible cancellation terms or complimentary rate benefits per the property's loyalty program terms; verify eligibility before applying modified policy terms.

**Group Block Attrition Disputes:** Handled directly between Sales/Catering leadership and the group's contracted representative, not by individual Reservations Agents.

**Force Majeure Cancellations (e.g., severe weather, travel advisories):** May warrant a policy exception to standard cancellation penalties at the discretion of the Director of Reservations or General Manager, applied consistently to all affected guests.

**VIP or Owner-Referred Guest Bookings:** No differentiated rate or inventory priority based on status alone; however, Guest Relations coordination is initiated per the VIP Guest Handling document (GR-SOP-401) once the reservation is confirmed.

**System Outage During Booking Window:** If the PMS or booking engine experiences downtime, Reservations Agents manually document pending bookings and enter them into the system once restored, honoring the rate and availability quoted at the time of the outage.

## 9. Troubleshooting

**Problem: A guest disputes a no-show charge, claiming they cancelled in time.**
- *Possible Causes:* Cancellation not properly processed, guest confusion about the cancellation deadline, genuine system error.
- *Resolution:* Review the reservation history and cancellation log for any record of the guest's claimed cancellation attempt.
- *Escalation:* If no record exists and the guest maintains the dispute, escalate to the Reservations Supervisor for final determination.

**Problem: A rate loaded incorrectly across a distribution channel, resulting in guest bookings at an unintended rate.**
- *Possible Causes:* Manual entry error, channel manager synchronization issue.
- *Resolution:* Correct the rate immediately across all channels; honor the incorrectly quoted rate for guests who already booked, per property policy on rate error handling.
- *Escalation:* Escalate to Revenue Management for review of the root cause and channel manager configuration.

**Problem: A group block is significantly under-pickup approaching the cutoff date.**
- *Possible Causes:* Overestimated group demand, competing event dates, insufficient promotion by the group organizer.
- *Resolution:* Notify Sales and Revenue Management to assess whether to extend the cutoff, renegotiate terms, or release excess inventory.
- *Escalation:* Director of Reservations and Sales leadership for contract-level decisions.

**Problem: The property is oversold with no walk candidates identified in time.**
- *Possible Causes:* Underestimated no-show/cancellation rate, late identification of the oversold situation.
- *Resolution:* Duty Manager and Front Office work through the walk policy immediately, prioritizing guest communication and comparable alternative accommodation.
- *Escalation:* Immediate involvement of the General Manager for any last-minute oversold situation lacking adequate lead time.

## 10. Frequently Asked Questions

**1. How do I know which rate code to apply to a reservation?**
Confirm the guest's eligibility (public, corporate, loyalty, promotional) against the rate code reference guide before applying any restricted rate.

**2. What is the difference between a guaranteed and non-guaranteed reservation?**
A guaranteed reservation is secured with a payment method and held regardless of arrival time, subject to a no-show charge if unused; a non-guaranteed reservation may be released at a defined cutoff time if not claimed.

**3. How do I process a cancellation for a guest calling within the penalty period?**
Inform the guest of the applicable cancellation penalty per the guarantee policy, process the cancellation, and provide a cancellation confirmation number.

**4. What should I do if I find a rate discrepancy between our website and an OTA listing?**
Report it immediately to Revenue Management for correction, as rate parity must be maintained across all distribution channels.

**5. How far in advance should Front Office be notified of an oversold date?**
At least 24 hours in advance where possible, allowing time for proactive resolution options.

**6. What is a group block cutoff date?**
The date by which unbooked rooms within a group block are released back into general inventory per the group's contract terms.

**7. Can I modify a reservation's dates without canceling and rebooking?**
Yes, standard date modifications are processed directly in the PMS, subject to availability and any rate change for the new dates.

**8. What happens to unclaimed rooms in a group block after the cutoff date?**
They are released back into general inventory for individual booking, unless an extension has been approved per the contract.

**9. How do I handle a guest requesting a rate match to a lower price they found elsewhere?**
Verify the competing rate's terms and eligibility; escalate to the Reservations Supervisor if a rate match falls outside standard Agent authority.

**10. What is attrition, and how does it affect a group block?**
Attrition is the percentage of contracted group rooms that must be picked up to avoid financial penalty; falling short of this threshold may trigger contractual penalties for the group.

**11. Can I waive a no-show charge if the guest has a compelling reason?**
Only within your authorized discretion per property policy; disputes beyond Agent authority are escalated to the Reservations Supervisor or Duty Manager.

**12. How is overbooking different from simply selling too many rooms by mistake?**
Overbooking is a controlled, strategic practice based on historical no-show and cancellation data, managed within approved thresholds set by Revenue Management, not an unintentional error.

**13. What information must I confirm with a guest before finalizing a phone reservation?**
Arrival/departure dates, room type, rate, guarantee/cancellation policy, and payment method, confirmed verbally before completing the booking.

**14. Who sets the overbooking percentage for a given date?**
Revenue Management, based on historical no-show and cancellation forecasting for that specific date range.

**15. What do I do if a guest's loyalty tier isn't reflected correctly in their reservation?**
Verify the guest's loyalty profile in the PMS and correct the reservation to reflect their accurate tier and associated benefits.

**16. How quickly should a written confirmation be sent after a phone booking?**
Within 5 minutes of completing the reservation.

**17. What should I do if a group organizer requests an extension to the cutoff date?**
Escalate the request to Sales and Revenue Management, as cutoff extensions require approval outside standard Reservations Agent authority.

**18. What happens if the PMS goes down while I'm processing a reservation?**
Document the booking manually with all relevant details and enter it into the system once it is restored, honoring the original quoted rate and availability.

**19. Can OTA bookings be modified directly by our Reservations team?**
Modification capability depends on the specific OTA's system integration; some changes may need to be processed through the OTA's platform directly per the channel agreement.

**20. What is the correct process if a guest is walked due to an oversold situation?**
Front Office and the Duty Manager identify a comparable alternative accommodation per the walk policy, and the walked guest and resolution details are documented for post-incident review.

## 13. Training Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Guest Disputes a No-Show Charge
**Situation:** A guest calls stating they cancelled their reservation three days before arrival but was still charged a no-show fee.

**Correct Response:** The Agent reviews the reservation and cancellation history for any record of the claimed cancellation, and if no record exists, calmly explains the findings while escalating to the Supervisor for final review given the guest's dispute.

**Incorrect Response:** Immediately refunding the charge without reviewing the reservation history, or dismissing the guest's claim without investigation.

**Supervisor Notes:** No-show disputes must always be resolved through documented reservation history review, not assumption in either direction.

**Learning Outcome:** Consistent, documentation-based dispute resolution protects both guest trust and revenue integrity.

---

### Scenario 2: Rate Loaded Incorrectly Across a Booking Channel
**Situation:** A significant rate loading error results in guests booking a suite category at the standard room rate through an OTA for two days before being discovered.

**Correct Response:** The error is corrected immediately across all channels, and guests who already booked at the incorrect rate are honored per property policy on rate error handling, while the root cause is reported to Revenue Management for channel manager review.

**Incorrect Response:** Cancelling the affected guests' reservations without honoring the originally quoted rate.

**Supervisor Notes:** Rate errors are a property-side responsibility; honoring the quoted rate for existing bookings protects guest trust even when the error was unintentional.

**Learning Outcome:** Rate integrity failures require prompt correction and fair handling of already-confirmed bookings, not guest-side penalty.

---

### Scenario 3: Group Block Significantly Under Pickup Near Cutoff
**Situation:** A corporate group block is at only 40% pickup two days before the contracted cutoff date, well below expected pace.

**Correct Response:** Reservations notifies Sales and Revenue Management immediately, allowing time to assess options such as contacting the group organizer, adjusting the cutoff, or releasing partial inventory strategically.

**Incorrect Response:** Allowing the cutoff date to pass without proactive communication, resulting in either lost inventory or an unmanaged attrition dispute.

**Supervisor Notes:** Early, proactive communication before a cutoff date provides far more resolution options than reactive handling afterward.

**Learning Outcome:** Continuous pickup monitoring and early escalation protect both revenue and the group relationship.
