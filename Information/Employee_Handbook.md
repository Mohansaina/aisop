## 1. Purpose

This Employee Handbook establishes the foundational policies, expectations, and standards governing employment at the property. It covers onboarding, workplace conduct, attendance, appearance and grooming standards, compensation and benefits overview, leave policies, workplace safety, disciplinary procedures, and separation from employment. This document ensures every employee understands their rights, responsibilities, and the behavioral and professional standards expected across all departments.

This handbook also serves as an authoritative reference within the AI Operations Assistant knowledge base for general employment policy questions, supplementing department-specific SOPs that govern operational task execution.

---

## 2. Scope

This handbook applies to all employees of the property, including full-time, part-time, seasonal, and temporary staff across all departments. It covers general employment policies, conduct standards, and administrative procedures common to all roles. It does not replace department-specific SOPs governing operational procedures (e.g., Front Desk Operations, Housekeeping, Food Safety), nor does it override applicable local, state, or federal employment law, which takes precedence in the event of any conflict.

---

## 3. Definitions

- **Onboarding**: The structured process of introducing a new employee to the property, including orientation, documentation, training, and initial role integration.
- **Introductory Period (also called "Probationary Period")**: The initial period of employment, typically 90 days, during which performance and fit are actively evaluated.
- **PTO (Paid Time Off)**: Accrued paid leave available for vacation, personal time, or other approved absences.
- **Progressive Discipline**: A structured disciplinary approach that generally escalates through verbal warning, written warning, final warning, and termination, applied based on the severity and frequency of the issue.
- **Grooming Standard**: The property's defined expectations for personal appearance, uniform, and hygiene while on duty.
- **Chain of Command**: The defined reporting structure through which employees escalate questions, concerns, or issues.
- **Open Door Policy**: The practice of allowing employees to raise concerns directly with management at any level without fear of retaliation.
- **Separation**: The conclusion of an employee's employment, whether voluntary (resignation) or involuntary (termination, layoff).
- **Harassment**: Unwelcome conduct based on a protected characteristic that creates a hostile, intimidating, or offensive work environment, strictly prohibited under this handbook and applicable law.
- **Conflict of Interest**: A situation where an employee's personal interests could improperly influence their professional judgment or duties.

## 4. Roles & Responsibilities

### All Employees
- Adhere to all policies outlined in this handbook and applicable department-specific SOPs.
- Report to work as scheduled, on time, and in appropriate uniform/grooming standard.
- Report any workplace safety concerns, policy violations, or conduct issues through the appropriate chain of command.
- Treat guests and colleagues with respect, professionalism, and courtesy at all times.

### Department Supervisors / Managers
- Conduct new employee onboarding and ongoing training within their department.
- Monitor attendance, performance, and conduct, addressing issues promptly and fairly.
- Apply progressive discipline consistently and document all disciplinary actions.
- Escalate policy violations requiring HR involvement (e.g., harassment complaints, serious misconduct) immediately.

### Human Resources
- Maintains employee records, benefits administration, and leave policy compliance.
- Investigates formal complaints, including harassment and discrimination reports.
- Ensures consistent, lawful application of disciplinary and separation procedures.
- Administers onboarding documentation and new hire orientation.

### General Manager / Executive Team
- Sets the overall standard of workplace culture and ensures departmental leadership models expected conduct.
- Reviews and approves significant disciplinary actions, including termination decisions.
- Ensures property-wide compliance with employment law and corporate policy.

## 5. Standard Operating Procedure

### Phase 1: Onboarding and Orientation

**Purpose:** To ensure every new employee is properly documented, trained, and integrated into their role and the property's culture.

**Required Materials:** New hire paperwork, orientation materials, department-specific training schedule, uniform/ID badge issuance.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. HR completes all required employment documentation (tax forms, employment eligibility verification, direct deposit setup) on or before the employee's first day.
2. New employee attends property-wide orientation covering company culture, core policies, safety procedures, and benefits overview.
3. Department Supervisor conducts role-specific orientation, including relevant SOP review and shadowing period.
4. Employee is issued uniform, ID badge, and any required access credentials.
5. Employee completes required initial training modules and certifications relevant to their role (e.g., food safety certification, PMS training).
6. Supervisor confirms completion of the Introductory Period checklist at 30, 60, and 90 days.

**Quality Checkpoints:** 100% of required documentation completed before first shift; role-specific training completed within the first two weeks of employment.

**Expected Outcome:** New employee is fully documented, trained, and prepared to perform their role safely and effectively.

**Escalation Conditions:** Any missing documentation or incomplete eligibility verification is escalated to HR immediately before the employee begins working.

---

### Phase 2: Attendance and Scheduling

**Purpose:** To maintain reliable staffing levels and ensure fair, consistent application of attendance policy.

**Required Materials:** Scheduling system, attendance tracking log, time-off request form.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Employees review published schedules and confirm availability in advance per department scheduling timelines.
2. Time-off requests are submitted through the designated system with reasonable advance notice per department policy.
3. Employees clock in/out accurately for each shift using the property's timekeeping system.
4. Any unplanned absence or late arrival is reported to the Supervisor as far in advance as possible, per property call-out procedure.
5. Supervisors track attendance patterns and address recurring issues per progressive discipline procedure.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Time-off requests processed within 3 business days of submission; attendance records reconciled weekly by Supervisors.

**Expected Outcome:** Consistent, predictable staffing levels supported by fair and transparent attendance management.

**Escalation Conditions:** Patterns of excessive absenteeism or tardiness are escalated to HR for formal documentation and progressive discipline consideration.

---

### Phase 3: Workplace Conduct and Appearance Standards

**Purpose:** To maintain a professional, respectful, and brand-consistent workplace environment.

**Required Materials:** Uniform and grooming standard guide, code of conduct policy.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Employees report to work in the complete, clean, and properly fitted uniform designated for their role.
2. Grooming standards (hair, jewelry, tattoos/visible ink policy, nails, fragrance) are followed per property-specific guidance provided at onboarding.
3. Employees treat guests, colleagues, and vendors with respect and professionalism at all times, avoiding conduct that could be perceived as discriminatory, harassing, or unprofessional.
4. Personal phone use is limited to designated break areas and times, not during active guest-facing duties.
5. Any conduct concern observed is reported through the chain of command or directly to HR.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Uniform and grooming compliance verified daily by Supervisors at shift start.

**Expected Outcome:** A consistently professional, respectful, and brand-aligned workplace environment.

**Escalation Conditions:** Repeated appearance or conduct standard violations are addressed through progressive discipline; any harassment or discrimination concern is escalated immediately to HR.

---

### Phase 4: Progressive Discipline

**Purpose:** To address performance or conduct issues fairly, consistently, and with appropriate documentation.

**Required Materials:** Disciplinary action form, documented performance/conduct history.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Supervisor addresses the issue directly with the employee as soon as it is identified, documenting the conversation.
2. For continued or more serious issues, a formal verbal warning is issued and documented.
3. Continued issues escalate to a written warning, clearly outlining the issue, expectation, and consequence of continued non-compliance.
4. Serious or continued issues may escalate to a final written warning, prepared in coordination with HR.
5. Termination decisions require HR and, where applicable, General Manager review and approval before being communicated to the employee.
6. All disciplinary steps are documented in the employee's personnel file.

**Quality Checkpoints:** 100% of disciplinary actions documented and placed in the employee file within 48 hours of occurrence.

**Expected Outcome:** Fair, consistent, and well-documented handling of performance and conduct issues.

**Escalation Conditions:** Any disciplinary matter involving suspected harassment, discrimination, safety violation, or legal exposure is escalated immediately to HR rather than handled solely at the department level.

---

### Phase 5: Separation from Employment

**Purpose:** To ensure a professional, compliant, and consistent process for voluntary or involuntary separation.

**Required Materials:** Resignation/termination documentation, final pay processing checklist, exit interview form.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. For voluntary resignation, employee provides notice per property policy (commonly two weeks) in writing to their Supervisor and HR.
2. For involuntary separation, HR and the Department Manager coordinate the termination meeting and required documentation in compliance with applicable law.
3. Employee returns all property assets (uniform, ID badge, keys, equipment) at the time of separation.
4. Final pay is processed in accordance with applicable law and property payroll schedule.
5. HR conducts an exit interview where applicable to gather feedback and confirm final administrative details.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Final pay processed within the legally required timeframe; all property assets collected and logged at separation.

**Expected Outcome:** A professional, compliant, and well-documented conclusion to the employment relationship.

**Escalation Conditions:** Any dispute regarding final pay, benefits continuation, or separation terms is escalated immediately to HR leadership for resolution.

## 7. Best Practices

- **Address performance and conduct issues promptly rather than allowing patterns to develop.** Early, direct conversations prevent minor issues from escalating into formal disciplinary matters.
- **Document consistently, even for informal conversations.** Clear documentation protects both the employee and the property by ensuring fair, transparent handling of any recurring issue.
- **Apply policy consistently across all employees and departments.** Inconsistent enforcement undermines trust in leadership and creates legal exposure for the property.
- **Encourage use of the Open Door Policy without hesitation.** Employees who feel safe raising concerns early help the property identify and resolve issues before they grow more serious.
- **Model the conduct and professionalism expected of all employees at every leadership level.** Culture is set far more by observed behavior than by written policy alone.

## 8. Exceptions

**Introductory Period Employees:** May be subject to modified benefits eligibility and a streamlined separation process during the initial 90-day period, consistent with applicable law and property policy.

**Seasonal / Temporary Employees:** Subject to the same conduct and safety standards as all employees, with modified scheduling and benefits eligibility per their specific employment classification.

**Medical or Family Leave:** Employees on approved leave (medical, family, or otherwise legally protected) are subject to specific job-protection and reinstatement rights per applicable law; HR manages these cases individually in compliance with legal requirements.

**Union-Represented Employees (where applicable):** Subject to the terms of the applicable collective bargaining agreement, which takes precedence over general handbook policy where a conflict exists.

**Emergency Situations:** Standard attendance and scheduling policy may be temporarily adjusted per Duty Manager or HR guidance during declared property emergencies (see Emergency Response Manual, ER-SOP-201).

## 9. Troubleshooting

**Problem: An employee repeatedly arrives late without adequate notice.**
- *Possible Causes:* Personal transportation issues, scheduling miscommunication, disengagement.
- *Resolution:* Supervisor addresses the pattern directly, documents each occurrence, and applies progressive discipline as needed.
- *Escalation:* Persistent issues are escalated to HR for formal documentation and further action.

**Problem: An employee reports feeling harassed by a coworker.**
- *Possible Causes:* Genuine misconduct, miscommunication, personality conflict.
- *Resolution:* Report is documented and escalated to HR immediately for a formal, impartial investigation; do not attempt to resolve informally at the department level for harassment allegations.
- *Escalation:* Immediate and mandatory HR involvement for any harassment or discrimination report.

**Problem: Two employees have a workplace conflict affecting team morale.**
- *Possible Causes:* Communication breakdown, unclear role boundaries, personal friction.
- *Resolution:* Supervisor facilitates a direct conversation between the employees, documenting outcomes and expectations going forward.
- *Escalation:* Unresolved or escalating conflicts are referred to HR for mediation support.

**Problem: An employee disputes a disciplinary action as unfair.**
- *Possible Causes:* Inconsistent application of policy, missing context, genuine documentation error.
- *Resolution:* Employee is encouraged to use the Open Door Policy to raise the concern with HR for review of the documentation and process followed.
- *Escalation:* HR reviews and, if warranted, corrects or upholds the original action with documented rationale.

## 10. Frequently Asked Questions

**1. How long is the Introductory Period?**
Typically 90 days from the employee's start date, during which performance and fit are actively evaluated.

**2. How do I request time off?**
Submit a request through the designated scheduling system with reasonable advance notice per department policy.

**3. What should I do if I'm going to be late or absent?**
Notify your Supervisor as far in advance as possible following the property's call-out procedure.

**4. What are the grooming standards for visible tattoos?**
Follow the property-specific grooming standard guide provided at onboarding, as policies vary by role and property brand standard.

**5. Can I use my personal phone during my shift?**
Personal phone use is limited to designated break areas and times, not during active guest-facing duties.

**6. What happens during the disciplinary process?**
Issues generally progress through verbal warning, written warning, final warning, and termination, based on severity and frequency, all documented per property policy.

**7. Who do I talk to if I experience or witness harassment?**
Report it immediately to Human Resources; this type of concern should not be handled informally at the department level.

**8. How much notice do I need to give if I resign?**
Property policy commonly requires two weeks' written notice, though specific requirements may vary by role.

**9. What happens to my final paycheck if I leave the company?**
Final pay is processed in accordance with applicable law and the property's payroll schedule.

**10. Can I appeal a disciplinary action I believe is unfair?**
Yes, using the Open Door Policy to raise the concern with Human Resources for review.

**11. What items do I need to return if I leave the company?**
All property assets, including uniform, ID badge, keys, and any issued equipment.

**12. Is there a dress code for days I'm not guest-facing?**
Department-specific guidance applies; confirm with your Supervisor whether a modified or business-casual standard applies to non-guest-facing roles.

**13. What is the Open Door Policy?**
It allows employees to raise concerns directly with management at any level without fear of retaliation.

**14. Do seasonal employees receive the same benefits as full-time staff?**
Benefits eligibility varies by employment classification; confirm specific eligibility details with Human Resources.

**15. What should I do if I have a conflict with a coworker?**
Attempt a direct, respectful conversation first where appropriate, and involve your Supervisor if the issue persists or affects work performance.

**16. How are 30/60/90-day reviews conducted?**
Your Supervisor will meet with you at each milestone to review performance, address questions, and confirm expectations going forward.

**17. What is considered a conflict of interest?**
A situation where your personal interests could improperly influence your professional judgment or duties; disclose any potential conflict to your Supervisor or HR.

**18. Can I be disciplined for something posted on my personal social media?**
Conduct that violates company policy, including harassment, discrimination, or disclosure of confidential information, may result in disciplinary action regardless of platform; consult the property's social media policy for specifics.

**19. What if I disagree with my Supervisor's account of a disciplinary conversation?**
You may document your own account and request HR review of the documentation and process followed.

**20. Who approves a termination decision?**
Human Resources and, where applicable, the General Manager review and approve termination decisions before they are communicated to the employee.

## 13. Training Scenarios

### Scenario 1: New Employee Struggling to Meet Uniform Standards
**Situation:** A newly hired Front Desk Agent has arrived out of uniform compliance (untucked shirt, missing name badge) on two separate shifts during their first week.

**Correct Response:** The Supervisor addresses the issue directly and privately, clarifies the grooming standard expectations, and documents the informal conversation without escalating immediately to formal discipline given the employee's short tenure.

**Incorrect Response:** Publicly reprimanding the employee in front of guests or coworkers, or ignoring the pattern without addressing it.

**Supervisor Notes:** Early-tenure issues are best addressed through direct, private coaching before considering formal disciplinary steps.

**Learning Outcome:** Correcting standards issues promptly and respectfully supports employee success without unnecessary escalation.

---

### Scenario 2: Employee Reports a Harassment Concern
**Situation:** An employee approaches their Supervisor to report that a coworker has made repeated unwelcome comments that make them uncomfortable.

**Correct Response:** The Supervisor listens respectfully, does not attempt to resolve the matter informally, and immediately escalates the report to Human Resources for a formal investigation.

**Incorrect Response:** Telling the employee to "work it out" with the coworker directly, or delaying the report to HR.

**Supervisor Notes:** Harassment concerns require mandatory, immediate HR involvement regardless of how minor the Supervisor perceives the issue to be.

**Learning Outcome:** Proper escalation of harassment concerns protects the employee, the accused party's due process, and the property's legal compliance.

---

### Scenario 3: Employee Disputes a Written Warning
**Situation:** An employee receives a written warning for attendance issues but believes several of the cited absences were pre-approved and should not count against them.

**Correct Response:** The employee is encouraged to raise the concern through the Open Door Policy with Human Resources, who reviews the attendance records and documentation before upholding or correcting the warning.

**Incorrect Response:** Dismissing the employee's concern without reviewing the underlying documentation.

**Supervisor Notes:** Disciplinary disputes must be reviewed against actual documentation rather than assumed to be correct by default.

**Learning Outcome:** Fair, documentation-based review processes maintain trust in the disciplinary system and ensure accuracy.
