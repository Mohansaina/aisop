## 1. Purpose

This Lost & Found Policy (also referred to as "Lost Property Policy" or "Found Item Policy") establishes the standardized process for logging, storing, tracking, claiming, and disposing of guest items found on property. It ensures every found item is handled with consistency, accountability, and transparency, protecting both guest trust and the property from liability. This document also defines retention periods, guest claim verification, high-value item handling, and shipping procedures for unclaimed or unreturned property.

This policy applies across all departments that may recover guest items — Housekeeping, Front Desk, Food & Beverage, Security, Valet, and Spa — and serves as an authoritative reference within the AI Operations Assistant knowledge base for lost and found employee questions.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees who may discover, receive, store, or release found guest property, including:

- Housekeeping (Room Attendants, Supervisors)
- Front Desk and Guest Services
- Food & Beverage staff
- Valet and Bell Staff
- Spa and Fitness Center staff
- Security / Loss Prevention

This policy covers the intake, logging, secure storage, guest claim process, high-value/perishable item handling, retention periods, and disposal or donation of unclaimed items. It does not cover guest billing disputes, room damage claims, or theft investigations involving employee misconduct, which are addressed in separate Security and HR procedures.

---

## 3. Definitions

- **Lost Property (also called "Lost and Found" or "Found Item")**: Any item left behind by a guest or visitor and recovered by an employee anywhere on property.
- **Intake Log**: The centralized record, physical or digital, documenting every found item, including description, location found, date, and finder.
- **Claim Verification**: The process of confirming a guest's identity and rightful ownership before releasing a found item.
- **High-Value Item**: Any found item exceeding a property-defined value threshold (commonly $200 or higher), including jewelry, electronics, and cash, requiring enhanced security storage and documentation.
- **Perishable Item**: Any found item subject to spoilage or safety concern, including food, medication, or liquids, which cannot be held for standard retention periods.
- **Retention Period**: The defined length of time a found item is held before disposal, donation, or destruction if unclaimed (commonly 90 days, longer for high-value items).
- **Chain of Custody**: The documented record of every individual who has handled a found item from intake through final disposition.
- **Shipping Fee**: The cost charged to a guest for returning a claimed item via mail or courier, typically billed to the guest's card on file or collected via prepaid label.

## 4. Roles & Responsibilities

### Room Attendant / Any Finding Employee
- Immediately reports any found item to their Supervisor or the Lost & Found custodian.
- Does not use, retain, or personally handle found items beyond secure transport to the intake point.
- Completes or assists in completing the Lost Property Form at the time of discovery.

### Housekeeping Supervisor
- Collects found items from Room Attendants at the end of each shift.
- Verifies items are logged accurately before transfer to the central Lost & Found storage.
- Ensures high-value items are transported directly to Security rather than left in interim storage.

### Front Desk / Guest Services
- Receives guest inquiries regarding lost items and checks the Lost & Found log.
- Facilitates claim verification and coordinates item release or shipping.
- Directs high-value item claims to Security for enhanced verification.

### Security / Loss Prevention
- Maintains custody of all high-value found items in a secured, access-controlled location.
- Oversees the centralized Intake Log and retention period compliance.
- Investigates any discrepancies, disputes, or suspected theft related to found property.
- Approves final disposition (donation, disposal, or destruction) of unclaimed items after the retention period.

### General Manager / Director of Security
- Approves policy exceptions and disputes escalated beyond Security's standard authority.
- Reviews periodic Lost & Found audit reports for compliance and pattern identification.

## 5. Standard Operating Procedure

### Phase 1: Item Discovery and Intake

**Purpose:** To ensure every found item is immediately and accurately documented from the moment of discovery.

**Required Materials:** Lost Property Form, secure bag or container, Intake Log (physical or digital system).

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Employee who finds an item does not use or examine its contents beyond what is necessary for identification.
2. Employee immediately reports the find to their Supervisor or the designated Lost & Found custodian.
3. Complete the Lost Property Form, documenting item description, exact location found, date/time, and finder's name.
4. Place the item in a secure bag or container labeled with a matching intake reference number.
5. Transport the item to the central Lost & Found storage location, or directly to Security if it qualifies as a high-value item.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Item logged within 15 minutes of discovery in 100% of cases.

**Expected Outcome:** Every found item has a documented, traceable record from the moment of discovery.

**Escalation Conditions:** If the item is cash, jewelry, electronics, or any item exceeding the high-value threshold, escalate directly to Security rather than routing through standard interim storage.

---

### Phase 2: Secure Storage and Log Management

**Purpose:** To maintain organized, secure custody of all found items pending guest claim or disposition.

**Required Materials:** Locked Lost & Found storage room or cabinet, centralized Intake Log, item labels.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Lost & Found custodian receives items from department Supervisors at scheduled intervals (minimum once daily).
2. Cross-check each item against its corresponding Lost Property Form entry in the Intake Log.
3. Store standard items in general Lost & Found storage, organized by intake date and reference number.
4. Store high-value items in the Security-controlled safe or locked cabinet with restricted access.
5. Update the Intake Log status to "In Storage" with location noted.

**Quality Checkpoints:** 100% of items cross-referenced and logged correctly within 24 hours of intake.

**Expected Outcome:** All found items are secured, organized, and retrievable through the Intake Log.

**Escalation Conditions:** Any item discovered missing from storage or with a broken chain of custody is escalated immediately to Security for investigation.

---

### Phase 3: Guest Claim and Verification

**Purpose:** To confirm rightful ownership before releasing any found item to a claiming guest.

**Required Materials:** Intake Log, guest identification, Claim Verification Form.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Guest contacts Front Desk, Guest Services, or the property directly to report a lost item.
2. Employee searches the Intake Log using item description, date, and location details provided by the guest.
3. If a potential match is found, ask the guest to describe additional identifying details not visible externally (e.g., contents of a bag, specific markings) to verify ownership.
4. For high-value items, require government-issued photo ID matching the guest's reservation or a verifiable description of unique item features.
5. Complete the Claim Verification Form and release the item, or arrange shipping if the guest has departed.
6. Update the Intake Log status to "Claimed" with release date and method noted.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Claim verification completed and documented in 100% of releases; no item released without a completed Claim Verification Form.

**Expected Outcome:** Items are returned only to verified, rightful owners, with a complete documentation trail.

**Escalation Conditions:** Any claim with uncertain or conflicting verification details is escalated to Security or the Duty Manager before release.

---

### Phase 4: Shipping Unclaimed-in-Person Items

**Purpose:** To return verified items to guests who have already departed the property.

**Required Materials:** Shipping materials, prepaid label system or guest payment method, Claim Verification Form.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Complete claim verification per Phase 3 procedures via phone or email.
2. Confirm shipping address and method with the guest.
3. Process the applicable shipping fee via the guest's card on file or a provided payment method.
4. Package the item securely and generate a shipping label with tracking.
5. Update the Intake Log with shipping date, carrier, and tracking number.
6. Provide the guest with tracking information via email or phone.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Verified claims shipped within 2 business days of guest confirmation and payment.

**Expected Outcome:** Guest receives their returned item with full tracking transparency.

**Escalation Conditions:** If a guest disputes the shipping fee, escalate to Front Office Supervisor or Duty Manager for policy clarification.

---

### Phase 5: Retention, Disposal, and Donation

**Purpose:** To responsibly manage unclaimed items after the defined retention period has expired.

**Required Materials:** Intake Log, disposition approval form.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Security reviews the Intake Log monthly for items exceeding the standard retention period (commonly 90 days; longer for high-value items per property policy).
2. Confirm no pending guest inquiry exists for the item before disposition.
3. Obtain Director of Security or General Manager approval for final disposition.
4. Dispose of, donate, or (for eligible high-value items) process per property policy on unclaimed valuables (e.g., employee raffle, charitable donation, or destruction).
5. Update the Intake Log with final disposition date and method.

**Quality Checkpoints:** 100% of disposed items have documented approval and updated log status.

**Expected Outcome:** Unclaimed items are responsibly and transparently processed, with no unresolved custody records.

**Escalation Conditions:** High-value or sentimental items are held for an extended review period at Security's discretion before final disposition.

## 7. Best Practices

- **Log items immediately, even if they seem insignificant.** A guest's perception of an item's importance may differ greatly from its apparent value; timely logging protects the property regardless of item type.
- **Never handle or use a found item beyond what is necessary for identification and secure storage.** This protects both the guest's property and the employee from any appearance of impropriety.
- **Always require verification beyond a simple description match for high-value items.** Verification detail thresholds should scale with item value to prevent fraudulent claims.
- **Maintain a single centralized Intake Log rather than department-specific lists.** Fragmented logs create gaps in chain of custody and slow guest claim resolution.
- **Communicate shipping fees transparently and upfront.** Guests are far more accepting of a fee disclosed proactively than one discovered after the fact.

## 8. Exceptions

**Perishable Items:** Food, medication, and similar perishable items cannot be held for the standard retention period; document and dispose of these items within 24–48 hours per property policy, noting disposal in the Intake Log.

**Cash:** Found cash is documented with exact amount, denomination breakdown where possible, and two-employee verification at intake; stored in the Security safe and subject to the high-value item retention period.

**Identification Documents (Passports, Driver's Licenses):** Do not treat as standard lost property; contact the guest directly using reservation contact information as soon as possible, given the urgency of travel document loss.

**Medication:** Attempt immediate guest contact given potential health urgency; if unclaimed, follow property policy on safe medication disposal.

**Items Suspected to Be Stolen or Involved in a Dispute:** Do not release to any claimant; escalate immediately to Security for investigation before any disposition decision.

**VIP Guest Items:** No differentiated storage or verification process; however, Guest Relations may proactively assist with expedited communication and shipping coordination.

## 9. Troubleshooting

**Problem: Two guests claim the same found item.**
- *Possible Causes:* Similar item descriptions, both guests genuinely believe the item is theirs, potential fraudulent claim.
- *Resolution:* Request additional unique identifying details from each claimant; do not release the item until ownership is clearly established.
- *Escalation:* Escalate to Security immediately for further investigation if ownership cannot be resolved through additional detail verification.

**Problem: A found item is missing from storage when a guest attempts to claim it.**
- *Possible Causes:* Logging error, misplaced item, potential theft.
- *Resolution:* Search all Lost & Found storage locations and cross-check the Intake Log chain of custody.
- *Escalation:* If the item cannot be located, escalate immediately to Security for formal investigation and guest communication per Duty Manager guidance.

**Problem: Guest disputes the condition of a returned item.**
- *Possible Causes:* Item damage occurred before or after intake, unclear condition documentation at intake.
- *Resolution:* Review Intake Log notes and any intake photos for original condition documentation.
- *Escalation:* Escalate to Security or Duty Manager if the dispute cannot be resolved through documentation review.

**Problem: A found high-value item has no property-defined retention guidance in the log.**
- *Possible Causes:* Log entry error, item value not clearly categorized at intake.
- *Resolution:* Escalate to Security to properly classify the item and apply the correct retention period.
- *Escalation:* Director of Security & Loss Prevention for classification disputes.

## 10. Frequently Asked Questions

**1. What should I do if I find an item in a guest room during cleaning?**
Do not use or examine the item beyond identification needs. Report it immediately to your Supervisor and complete the Lost Property Form.

**2. How long does the property hold onto lost items?**
Standard items are held for 90 days; high-value items are held longer per property policy, typically 180 days.

**3. What counts as a "high-value item"?**
Items exceeding the property-defined value threshold, commonly $200 or more, including jewelry, electronics, and cash.

**4. Can I personally return a found item directly to a guest without going through the log process?**
No. All found items must be logged and processed through the standard intake procedure regardless of how quickly the rightful owner is identified.

**5. What information do I need to claim a lost item?**
A description matching the Intake Log entry, and for high-value items, government-issued photo ID or additional unique identifying details.

**6. Is there a fee to have a lost item shipped back to me?**
Yes, a shipping fee applies and is communicated to the guest before shipment is processed.

**7. What happens to items that are never claimed?**
After the retention period expires and disposition approval is obtained, items are donated, disposed of, or processed per property policy on unclaimed valuables.

**8. What do I do if I find cash?**
Document the exact amount with a second employee present for verification, and transfer it directly to Security for safe storage.

**9. Can a guest claim an item over the phone without visiting in person?**
Yes, provided claim verification is completed per policy; the item can then be shipped to the guest.

**10. What should I do if I find a passport or ID document?**
Contact the guest directly using their reservation information as soon as possible, given the urgency of travel document loss, rather than waiting for the guest to inquire.

**11. Do I need to fill out a form for every single item, even small things like a phone charger?**
Yes. All found items, regardless of perceived value, must be logged via the Lost Property Form.

**12. What if a found item is perishable, like food or medication?**
Perishable items cannot be held for the standard retention period; document and dispose of them within 24–48 hours per property policy.

**13. Can I keep an unclaimed item for myself after the retention period?**
No. Disposition of unclaimed items follows the approved property policy (donation, disposal, or authorized process); items are never personally retained by staff.

**14. What if two guests claim the same lost item?**
Do not release the item to either claimant until ownership is clearly verified through additional identifying details; escalate to Security if unresolved.

**15. How do I handle a found item at the pool or fitness center?**
Follow the same intake procedure as any other department — log the item and transfer it to the central Lost & Found storage or Security if high-value.

**16. What if the guest's description doesn't quite match the item we have?**
Do not release the item; ask clarifying questions and escalate to Security if verification remains uncertain.

**17. Is there a different process for valet-recovered items found in guest vehicles?**
Yes, items found in vehicles follow the same intake and logging process, with the finding Valet employee completing the Lost Property Form immediately.

**18. What if a guest wants to donate their unclaimed lost item back to the hotel?**
Document the guest's confirmation in writing or verbally with a note in the log, and process per the property's donation procedure.

**19. Can I discuss details of what an item looks like before the guest describes it?**
No. Always ask the guest to describe the item first to help verify legitimate ownership rather than confirming details that could be guessed.

**20. Who approves final disposal of unclaimed items?**
The Director of Security & Loss Prevention or General Manager must approve final disposition after the retention period has passed.

## 13. Training Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Room Attendant Finds Jewelry During Departure Cleaning
**Situation:** A Room Attendant finds a gold necklace on the nightstand while cleaning a departed guest's room.

**Correct Response:** The Attendant does not handle the item beyond securing it, immediately reports the find to the Supervisor, completes the Lost Property Form, and the item is transferred directly to Security as a high-value item.

**Incorrect Response:** Placing the item in the standard Lost & Found bin without flagging it as high-value, or delaying the report until end of shift.

**Supervisor Notes:** High-value items require immediate escalation and enhanced chain-of-custody handling, not standard routing.

**Learning Outcome:** Value classification at the moment of discovery determines the correct handling path and protects both the guest and the property.

---

### Scenario 2: Guest Calls From Home Claiming a Lost Phone Charger
**Situation:** A guest calls two days after departure, describing a white phone charger left in their room.

**Correct Response:** Front Desk searches the Intake Log by room number and date, confirms the matching item and description, completes the Claim Verification Form, and arranges shipping with the applicable fee disclosed upfront.

**Incorrect Response:** Shipping the item immediately without any verification, or refusing to assist because the item seems low-value.

**Supervisor Notes:** Every item, regardless of value, follows the same verification and documentation standard.

**Learning Outcome:** Consistent process application, not item value, determines the correct claim handling procedure.

---

### Scenario 3: Item Missing From Storage at Time of Guest Claim
**Situation:** A guest arrives to claim a jacket logged three weeks prior, but the item cannot be located in storage.

**Correct Response:** The employee apologizes, searches all storage locations, reviews the Intake Log chain of custody, and escalates immediately to Security for formal investigation while keeping the guest informed.

**Incorrect Response:** Telling the guest the item was "probably thrown out" without investigation, or delaying escalation.

**Supervisor Notes:** Missing items require immediate, documented investigation regardless of how routine the situation may seem.

**Learning Outcome:** Chain-of-custody integrity must be actively investigated whenever a discrepancy is discovered, protecting both guest trust and property accountability.
