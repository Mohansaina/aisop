## 1. Purpose

This VIP Guest Handling document (also referred to as "VIP Servicing," "VIP Protocol," or "Elevated Guest Handling") establishes the standardized process for identifying, preparing for, welcoming, and servicing VIP guests throughout their stay. It ensures that every VIP arrival — whether a loyalty-tier elite member, a celebrity or public figure, a corporate executive, a repeat high-value guest, or an owner/ownership-referred guest — receives a consistently elevated, personalized, and discreet level of service aligned with brand standards.

This document also defines VIP classification tiers, coordination between departments, amenity standards, and privacy/discretion requirements, and serves as an authoritative reference within the AI Operations Assistant knowledge base for VIP-related employee questions.

---

## 2. Scope

This document applies to all employees who may interact with or support the preparation for a VIP guest, including:

- Guest Relations
- Front Desk and Front Office Management
- Housekeeping
- Concierge and Bell Staff
- Food & Beverage
- Security / Loss Prevention
- Valet

This document covers VIP identification and classification, pre-arrival preparation, arrival and in-house servicing, departure procedures, and privacy/discretion standards. It does not cover general Front Desk check-in/check-out procedures for non-VIP guests (see FO-SOP-102) or standard Housekeeping servicing procedures (see HK-SOP-201), which are addressed separately, though this document supplements both for VIP-specific handling.

## 3. Definitions

- **VIP Guest**: A guest designated for elevated service due to loyalty tier, reservation type, executive status, public profile, or special company/ownership designation.
- **VIP Tier**: The property's internal classification system (commonly VIP-1 through VIP-4, or similarly named tiers) indicating the level of elevated service required.
- **Guest Profile**: The PMS record containing a guest's preferences, stay history, special requests, and VIP designation.
- **Amenity**: A complimentary gift or gesture (e.g., fruit basket, champagne, personalized note) placed in a VIP guest's room prior to arrival.
- **Pre-Arrival Brief (also called "VIP Arrival Report" or "Daily VIP List")**: The daily report distributed to relevant departments identifying all VIP arrivals and their specific requirements.
- **Discretion Protocol**: The set of practices staff follow to protect the privacy of VIP guests, particularly public figures, including restricted information sharing and limited public acknowledgment.
- **Owner-Referred Guest**: A guest whose stay has been specifically referred or requested by hotel ownership or executive leadership, requiring elevated attention regardless of loyalty tier.
- **Site Inspection VIP**: A VIP designation applied to guests evaluating the property for future group or event bookings, requiring a heightened showcase-level experience.

## 4. Roles & Responsibilities

### Guest Relations
- Maintains and distributes the daily Pre-Arrival Brief / VIP Arrival Report.
- Coordinates amenity selection and placement with Housekeeping and Food & Beverage.
- Personally welcomes VIP guests upon arrival where operationally appropriate.
- Serves as the primary point of contact for VIP guests throughout their stay.

### Front Desk / Front Office Management
- Ensures VIP rooms are pre-assigned, pre-verified, and blocked from reassignment.
- Facilitates expedited or off-site/in-room check-in for VIP guests as appropriate to tier.
- Flags VIP profiles clearly in the PMS to alert all staff interacting with the reservation.

### Housekeeping
- Prioritizes VIP room servicing per the Housekeeping SOP priority cleaning procedure.
- Places approved amenities in the room prior to arrival, verified against the Pre-Arrival Brief.
- Applies enhanced quality inspection standards to VIP rooms.

### Concierge / Bell Staff
- Provides personalized luggage handling and room escort for VIP arrivals.
- Coordinates transportation, reservations, and itinerary requests specific to the VIP guest.

### Food & Beverage
- Prepares any requested in-room dining amenities or reservations coordinated through Guest Relations.
- Applies discretion protocols when VIP guests dine in public outlets.

### Security / Loss Prevention
- Assesses and coordinates any additional security needs for high-profile VIP guests.
- Enforces discretion protocols, including restricting room number or itinerary disclosure to unauthorized individuals.

### General Manager / Executive Team
- Personally engages with the highest VIP tiers (e.g., owner-referred guests, celebrity guests) as appropriate.
- Approves any non-standard amenity or service arrangement exceeding normal authority levels.

## 5. Standard Operating Procedure

### Phase 1: VIP Identification and Classification

**Purpose:** To accurately identify and classify incoming VIP guests so appropriate service levels can be prepared in advance.

**Required Materials:** PMS guest profile system, loyalty program tier data, reservation notes, ownership/executive referral communications.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Reservations or Guest Relations reviews incoming reservations daily against loyalty tier data, repeat guest history, and any special referral notes.
2. Classify each identified VIP guest according to the property's internal VIP Tier system.
3. Flag the guest profile in the PMS with the appropriate VIP designation, visible to all relevant departments.
4. Note any special requirements (privacy needs, dietary preferences, accessibility needs, celebration occasions) in the guest profile.

**Quality Checkpoints:** All VIP arrivals classified and flagged in the PMS at least 48 hours prior to arrival where reservation timing allows.

**Expected Outcome:** Every VIP guest is correctly identified and visible to all departments well ahead of arrival.

**Escalation Conditions:** Same-day VIP identification (e.g., a booking made under a non-obvious name) is escalated immediately to Guest Relations and Front Office Supervisor upon discovery.

---

### Phase 2: Pre-Arrival Preparation

**Purpose:** To ensure all departments are aligned and fully prepared before the VIP guest arrives.

**Required Materials:** Pre-Arrival Brief / VIP Arrival Report, amenity order forms, room assignment sheet.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Guest Relations distributes the daily Pre-Arrival Brief to Front Desk, Housekeeping, Food & Beverage, Concierge, and Security by 10:00 AM.
2. Confirm room assignment matches guest preferences and is blocked from reassignment in the PMS.
3. Coordinate amenity selection appropriate to the VIP tier and any known guest preferences or special occasion.
4. Housekeeping prioritizes the room for cleaning and amenity placement per priority cleaning procedures.
5. Concierge confirms any pre-arrival requests (transportation, dining reservations, special arrangements).
6. Security reviews any additional privacy or safety considerations for high-profile guests.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Pre-Arrival Brief distributed by 10:00 AM daily; VIP room verified ready no later than 1:00 PM on arrival day.

**Expected Outcome:** All departments are fully coordinated and the VIP guest's room and requests are prepared well ahead of arrival.

**Escalation Conditions:** If any element of preparation cannot be completed on schedule, escalate immediately to Guest Relations and the Duty Manager.

---

### Phase 3: Arrival and Welcome

**Purpose:** To deliver a seamless, personalized, and expedited arrival experience appropriate to the guest's VIP tier.

**Required Materials:** Pre-encoded key packet, welcome amenity, guest profile notes.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Guest Relations or a designated Manager personally greets the VIP guest upon arrival where tier and operational feasibility allow.
2. Offer expedited or off-desk check-in (e.g., in-room, lounge, or curbside registration) for appropriate VIP tiers.
3. Escort the guest to their room, highlighting relevant amenities and confirming any special requests have been fulfilled.
4. Confirm the guest's satisfaction with the room and amenities before departing.
5. Provide direct contact information for Guest Relations for the duration of the stay.

**Quality Checkpoints:** VIP welcome and check-in completed within 3 minutes of arrival for top-tier VIPs; personalized greeting delivered in 100% of VIP arrivals.

**Expected Outcome:** VIP guest experiences a seamless, personalized arrival that reflects their elevated status.

**Escalation Conditions:** If the room or any pre-arranged element is not ready upon VIP arrival, escalate immediately to the Duty Manager for real-time service recovery.

---

### Phase 4: In-House VIP Servicing

**Purpose:** To maintain a consistently elevated, attentive, and discreet level of service throughout the VIP guest's stay.

**Required Materials:** Guest profile, service request log, discretion protocol guidelines.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Guest Relations checks in with the VIP guest periodically (in person, by phone, or by note) without being intrusive.
2. All service requests from VIP guests are prioritized and tracked to completion with expedited follow-up.
3. Staff apply discretion protocols at all times, never disclosing room number, itinerary, or stay details to unauthorized individuals.
4. Any special occasion (birthday, anniversary, business milestone) is proactively acknowledged per guest profile notes.
5. Departments coordinate directly with Guest Relations for any additional requests arising during the stay.

**Quality Checkpoints:** VIP service requests resolved with priority handling and within compressed response-time targets relative to standard requests.

**Expected Outcome:** VIP guest receives attentive, discreet, and consistently elevated service throughout their stay.

**Escalation Conditions:** Any privacy breach, unauthorized disclosure, or service failure involving a VIP guest is escalated immediately to the Duty Manager and Director of Guest Relations.

---

### Phase 5: VIP Departure

**Purpose:** To conclude the VIP guest's stay with the same elevated, personalized attention shown at arrival.

**Required Materials:** Folio, departure gift (if applicable), guest profile notes.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Guest Relations or a designated Manager coordinates a personalized farewell where appropriate to VIP tier.
2. Offer expedited or off-desk checkout consistent with the arrival experience.
3. Review the folio for accuracy with particular attention to any comped or pre-arranged charges.
4. Provide a departure amenity or personalized note for top-tier VIPs where applicable.
5. Update the guest profile with any preferences or notes observed during the stay for future visits.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Guest profile updated within 24 hours of departure in 100% of VIP stays.

**Expected Outcome:** VIP guest departs with a consistently elevated impression and an updated profile supporting future personalization.

**Escalation Conditions:** Any unresolved billing or service issue at VIP departure is escalated immediately to the Duty Manager before the guest leaves, if possible.

## 7. Best Practices

- **Personalize without over-familiarity.** Referencing a guest's known preferences shows attentiveness, but excessive familiarity can feel intrusive; calibrate tone to the individual guest's demeanor.
- **Apply discretion protocols consistently, not selectively.** Privacy protection should never depend on staff judgment of "how famous" a guest is; all VIP profiles receive the same discretion standard.
- **Coordinate cross-departmentally before, not during, the stay.** Reactive coordination during a VIP stay creates visible service gaps; proactive Pre-Arrival Brief distribution prevents this.
- **Treat every VIP interaction as an opportunity to deepen loyalty, not just fulfill a transaction.** VIP guests often represent a disproportionate share of repeat revenue; consistent elevated service directly supports retention.
- **Document preferences observed during the stay, not just those explicitly requested.** Passive observation (e.g., preferred newspaper, room temperature) captured in the guest profile enables even stronger personalization on future visits.

## 8. Exceptions

**Celebrity or Public Figure Guests:** Require enhanced discretion protocols, including restricted staff awareness on a need-to-know basis and coordination with Security on access control; standard VIP amenity and service standards otherwise apply.

**Owner-Referred Guests:** Receive elevated attention regardless of loyalty tier status; General Manager or Executive Team involvement is expected for arrival and departure where feasible.

**Site Inspection VIPs:** Require a showcase-level experience highlighting event and meeting spaces; Sales and Catering coordinates directly with Guest Relations for this guest type.

**VIP Guests with Disabilities:** Accessibility needs are addressed with the same priority as VIP servicing; accessible room features must be verified functional in addition to standard VIP amenity preparation.

**Last-Minute or Unidentified VIP Arrivals:** If VIP status is discovered upon arrival rather than in advance, Front Desk immediately notifies Guest Relations and Duty Manager to initiate expedited in-house preparation.

**Group VIPs (e.g., Executive Delegations):** Coordinated as a block with a single point of contact from Guest Relations, while maintaining individual profile-level personalization where possible.

## 9. Troubleshooting

**Problem: VIP guest's pre-assigned room becomes unavailable due to a maintenance issue.**
- *Possible Causes:* Late-discovered maintenance failure, prior guest holdover.
- *Resolution:* Immediately identify a comparable or superior alternate room, notify Guest Relations and the Duty Manager, and personally inform the guest with an appropriate service recovery gesture.
- *Escalation:* Duty Manager approval required for any upgrade or compensation offered.

**Problem: Amenity was not placed in the VIP room prior to arrival.**
- *Possible Causes:* Miscommunication between Guest Relations and Housekeeping, late Pre-Arrival Brief distribution.
- *Resolution:* Arrange immediate delivery of the amenity with a personal apology from Guest Relations.
- *Escalation:* Recurring amenity failures are reported to the Director of Guest Relations for process review.

**Problem: Staff member inadvertently discloses a VIP guest's room number or presence to an unauthorized individual.**
- *Possible Causes:* Lack of discretion protocol awareness, high-pressure inquiry from a persistent third party.
- *Resolution:* Notify Security and the Duty Manager immediately; assess any resulting privacy or safety risk to the guest.
- *Escalation:* Any privacy breach is escalated to the Director of Guest Relations and Director of Security & Loss Prevention for formal review.

**Problem: VIP guest expresses dissatisfaction despite full protocol compliance.**
- *Possible Causes:* Unmet unstated expectation, external factors unrelated to service delivery, miscommunication.
- *Resolution:* Guest Relations personally engages to understand the concern and offers an appropriate, proportionate service recovery gesture.
- *Escalation:* Duty Manager or General Manager involvement for top-tier VIP dissatisfaction.

## 10. Frequently Asked Questions

**1. How is a guest classified as a VIP?**
Through loyalty tier status, repeat stay history, executive or ownership referral, or special designation reviewed by Reservations or Guest Relations during pre-arrival review.

**2. Where can I find the daily VIP arrival list?**
The Pre-Arrival Brief / VIP Arrival Report is distributed by Guest Relations to relevant departments by 10:00 AM daily.

**3. Can I tell another guest that a celebrity is staying at the property?**
No. Discretion protocols strictly prohibit disclosing any VIP guest's presence, room number, or itinerary to unauthorized individuals, including other guests.

**4. What should I do if a VIP guest arrives and I wasn't aware of their status?**
Notify Guest Relations and the Duty Manager immediately so expedited in-house preparation can begin.

**5. Are all VIP guests treated exactly the same?**
No. VIP Tiers determine the specific level of service, amenities, and personalization appropriate to each guest classification.

**6. Who is responsible for selecting the VIP welcome amenity?**
Guest Relations coordinates amenity selection based on VIP tier and any known guest preferences, in collaboration with Housekeeping and Food & Beverage.

**7. What if a VIP guest's room is not ready upon arrival?**
Escalate immediately to the Duty Manager and Guest Relations; offer an appropriate alternative and service recovery gesture while the room is finalized.

**8. Can Front Desk process a VIP check-in the same way as a standard guest?**
Top-tier VIPs typically receive expedited or off-desk check-in; Front Desk should follow the specific arrival process outlined in the Pre-Arrival Brief for each guest.

**9. What should I do if a VIP guest requests something outside normal policy?**
Escalate to Guest Relations or the Duty Manager, who can evaluate and approve reasonable accommodations within their authority.

**10. How soon after a VIP guest's stay should their profile be updated?**
Within 24 hours of departure, capturing any preferences or notes observed during the stay.

**11. Is security coordination required for every VIP guest?**
No. Enhanced security coordination is reserved for high-profile guests (e.g., celebrities, public figures) where discretion or safety considerations apply.

**12. What do I do if a media outlet calls asking to confirm a VIP guest's stay?**
Do not confirm or deny any guest's presence; direct all such inquiries to the General Manager or designated communications representative.

**13. Can a VIP guest's amenity be substituted if they have dietary restrictions?**
Yes. Guest Relations coordinates with Food & Beverage to select an appropriate alternative amenity respecting any noted dietary restrictions.

**14. How is an owner-referred guest different from a loyalty-tier VIP?**
Owner-referred guests receive elevated attention based on an ownership or executive relationship, regardless of their loyalty program tier status.

**15. What happens if a VIP guest checks in without any advance notice (walk-in VIP)?**
Front Desk immediately notifies Guest Relations and the Duty Manager to initiate real-time VIP preparation to the extent operationally feasible.

**16. Should I mention a VIP guest's status to my coworkers outside of those directly involved in servicing them?**
No. VIP information is shared strictly on a need-to-know basis among staff directly responsible for preparing or delivering service to that guest.

**17. What is the expected response time for a VIP service request compared to a standard guest?**
VIP requests are prioritized and handled on an expedited basis, faster than standard response-time targets, per department-specific priority procedures.

**18. Can I take a photo with a VIP guest if they seem open to it?**
No, unless the guest explicitly and directly initiates the request; staff should never request photos, autographs, or personal engagement beyond professional service delivery.

**19. What should Housekeeping do differently for a VIP room versus a standard room?**
Follow the same cleaning and quality standards, but prioritize the room in the servicing queue and ensure approved amenities are placed and verified per the Pre-Arrival Brief.

**20. Who approves a non-standard VIP service arrangement, like a private event space setup?**
The Director of Guest Relations or General Manager approves any arrangement exceeding standard VIP protocol authority.

## 13. Training Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Amenity Missing Upon VIP Arrival
**Situation:** A top-tier VIP guest arrives to find their room amenity was never placed due to a communication gap between Guest Relations and Housekeeping.

**Correct Response:** Guest Relations immediately apologizes, arranges prompt delivery of the amenity along with a thoughtful additional gesture, and documents the gap for internal process review.

**Incorrect Response:** Ignoring the miss or waiting for the guest to mention it before taking corrective action.

**Supervisor Notes:** Proactive acknowledgment and swift correction matter more to VIP guests than the original miss itself.

**Learning Outcome:** Service recovery speed and sincerity directly shape the guest's overall impression, even when a preparation step is missed.

---

### Scenario 2: Persistent Third Party Asking About a Guest's Presence
**Situation:** An unfamiliar individual repeatedly asks the Front Desk to confirm whether a specific well-known guest is staying at the property.

**Correct Response:** The Agent politely declines to confirm or deny any guest's presence, and discreetly notifies Security if the inquiry becomes persistent or concerning.

**Incorrect Response:** Confirming the guest's presence or room number to end the conversation quickly.

**Supervisor Notes:** Discretion protocols apply regardless of how insistent or convincing an inquiry seems.

**Learning Outcome:** Guest privacy protection is non-negotiable and must be maintained even under social pressure.

---

### Scenario 3: Walk-In Guest Turns Out to Be an Owner Referral
**Situation:** A guest arrives without a reservation, and during check-in it becomes clear they were personally referred by hotel ownership, though this was not communicated in advance.

**Correct Response:** The Front Desk Agent immediately notifies Guest Relations and the Duty Manager, who coordinate real-time VIP-level preparation and personally engage with the guest as soon as possible.

**Incorrect Response:** Processing the guest as a standard walk-in without escalating the ownership referral information.

**Supervisor Notes:** Any indication of an owner or executive referral warrants immediate escalation regardless of how the guest arrived.

**Learning Outcome:** VIP identification must remain active and responsive throughout every guest interaction, not only during pre-arrival review.
