## 1. Purpose

This procedure defines the precise, step-by-step actions required to check guests into and out of the hotel, covering identity verification, payment processing, room assignment, key issuance, folio management, and departure processing. It exists to ensure every guest arrival and departure transaction — whether a standard check-in, an early check-in, a walk-in registration, a standard checkout, an express checkout, or a late checkout/extended stay/checkout extension/delayed departure — is handled consistently, accurately, and within brand-defined service timeframes.

This document supplements the Front Desk Operations SOP (FO-SOP-101) by providing granular, transaction-level detail specific to arrival and departure processing, and serves as an authoritative reference within the AI Operations Assistant knowledge base.

---

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to all Front Desk Agents, Guest Service Agents, Front Office Supervisors, Duty Managers, and Night Audit staff who process guest arrivals or departures at the property. It covers:

- Reservation retrieval and verification
- Guest identification and registration
- Payment authorization and folio setup
- Room assignment and key issuance
- Check-in variants: standard, early, walk-in, group, VIP
- Check-out variants: standard, express, late/extended, group

It does not cover Reservations department booking creation, Housekeeping room-turnover procedures, or Night Audit financial close processes, which are addressed in separate SOPs.

## 3. Definitions

- **Registration Card**: The physical or digital record capturing guest identification, contact information, payment details, and signature authorizing charges.
- **Incidental Hold (also called "Authorization Hold")**: A pre-authorization placed on a guest's payment card to cover anticipated incidental charges beyond the room rate.
- **Rate Discrepancy**: A mismatch between the rate quoted to the guest at booking and the rate reflected in the PMS at check-in.
- **Walk-In Guest**: A guest arriving without an existing reservation who requests same-day accommodation.
- **Group Check-In**: A check-in process for guests arriving as part of a pre-negotiated group block, typically with pre-assigned rooms and pre-established billing arrangements.
- **Express Checkout**: A checkout method allowing guests to depart without visiting the Front Desk, typically via pre-authorized folio settlement and receipt delivered by email.
- **Late Checkout (also referred to as "Extended Stay," "Checkout Extension," or "Delayed Departure")**: An approved arrangement allowing a guest to remain in their room beyond the standard 11:00 AM checkout time.
- **Early Check-In**: An approved arrangement allowing a guest to occupy their room before the standard 3:00 PM check-in time.
- **Folio**: The itemized account record of charges and payments associated with a guest's stay.
- **Key Packet**: The prepared set of materials — key cards, welcome letter, property map — issued to guests at check-in.
- **Room Status Codes**: PMS indicators reflecting a room's readiness, including "Vacant Clean," "Vacant Dirty," "Vacant Inspected," and "Occupied."

## 4. Roles & Responsibilities

### Front Desk Agent
- Executes all standard check-in and check-out transactions.
- Verifies guest identity and payment method.
- Issues and deactivates room keys.
- Reviews folio charges with guests at departure.

### Front Office Supervisor
- Approves early check-in and late checkout requests beyond standard Agent authority.
- Resolves rate discrepancies and billing disputes exceeding Agent authorization limits.
- Oversees group check-in logistics.

### Duty Manager
- Approves compensation, comps, or walk decisions related to check-in/check-out issues.
- Makes final decisions on oversold situations affecting arriving guests.

### Housekeeping
- Updates room status in real time to support check-in room-readiness verification.
- Prioritizes turnover for rooms tied to early check-in or late checkout requests.

### Reservations
- Provides pre-arrival reservation accuracy, including rate codes, special requests, and group block details, feeding directly into check-in processing.

### Accounting / Night Audit
- Reconciles closed folios from checkouts and manages post-stay billing disputes.

## 5. Standard Operating Procedure

### Phase 1: Reservation Retrieval and Verification (Check-In)

**Purpose:** To confirm the guest's reservation details are accurate before beginning registration.

**Required Materials:** PMS terminal, confirmation number or guest name, government-issued ID.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Ask the guest for their name or confirmation number.
2. Retrieve the reservation in the PMS.
3. Confirm arrival date, departure date, room type, and rate.
4. Cross-check the rate quoted against the rate loaded in the PMS to identify any rate discrepancy.
5. Review any special requests or traces attached to the reservation.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Reservation located and verified within 60 seconds under normal system conditions.

**Expected Outcome:** An accurate, verified reservation ready for registration.

**Escalation Conditions:** If a rate discrepancy is identified, escalate to Front Office Supervisor before proceeding with payment collection.

---

### Phase 2: Guest Registration and Identification (Check-In)

**Purpose:** To formally register the guest and confirm their identity in compliance with property and regulatory requirements.

**Required Materials:** ID scanner or manual ID review, registration card (physical or digital), pen or digital signature pad.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Request a valid government-issued photo ID from the guest.
2. Confirm the name on the ID matches the reservation name (or is an authorized additional guest).
3. Complete or confirm the registration card with current contact information.
4. Obtain guest signature (physical or digital) authorizing charges and confirming rate acknowledgment.
5. Note the number of guests and any additional occupants for the stay.

**Quality Checkpoints:** 100% of registrations include verified ID and signed authorization prior to key issuance.

**Expected Outcome:** A fully compliant, signed registration record on file for the guest's stay.

**Escalation Conditions:** If a guest cannot provide valid ID, escalate to Front Office Supervisor for property-specific alternate verification policy.

---

### Phase 3: Payment Authorization (Check-In)

**Purpose:** To secure guaranteed payment for room charges and anticipated incidentals.

**Required Materials:** Credit card terminal, PMS terminal.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Request the guest's payment card.
2. Process an incidental hold in accordance with property policy (typically room rate plus a fixed incidental buffer per night).
3. Confirm authorization approval before proceeding.
4. For cash-paying guests, collect an appropriate cash deposit per property policy and document it on the folio.
5. Attach the payment method to the guest folio in the PMS.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Authorization confirmed and folio updated before key issuance in 100% of transactions.

**Expected Outcome:** A guaranteed payment method securely attached to the guest's account.

**Escalation Conditions:** If payment is declined and no alternate method is available, escalate to Front Office Supervisor immediately.

---

### Phase 4: Room Assignment and Key Issuance (Check-In)

**Purpose:** To assign an appropriate, ready room and provide functioning access credentials to the guest.

**Required Materials:** PMS terminal, key card encoder.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Confirm room status is "Vacant Inspected" or "Vacant Clean" before assignment.
2. Assign room based on guest preferences and reservation requirements (e.g., accessibility needs, bed type).
3. Encode key cards, matching the quantity to the number of registered guests.
4. Test key functionality if a key testing device is available at the desk.
5. Provide key packet including key cards, welcome letter, and Wi-Fi credentials.
6. Update room status to "Occupied" in the PMS.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Room status verified as ready prior to key issuance in 100% of check-ins.

**Expected Outcome:** Guest receives functioning room access and clear directional guidance.

**Escalation Conditions:** If no ready room matching the reservation is available, escalate to Front Office Supervisor for alternate accommodation or compensation decision.

---

### Phase 5: Folio Review (Check-Out)

**Purpose:** To ensure the guest departs with a fully accurate and understood account of charges.

**Required Materials:** PMS terminal, printer.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Retrieve the guest's folio using name, room number, or confirmation number.
2. Review all charges line-by-line with the guest, including room rate, taxes, and incidentals.
3. Address and resolve any discrepancies raised by the guest.
4. Confirm the guest has no additional pending charges (e.g., minibar, late room service).

**Quality Checkpoints:** All charges reviewed and confirmed with guest prior to payment processing in 100% of standard checkouts.

**Expected Outcome:** Guest has a fully transparent, agreed-upon final account.

**Escalation Conditions:** Disputes exceeding Agent's authorized adjustment limit are escalated to Supervisor or Duty Manager.

---

### Phase 6: Payment Settlement and Departure (Check-Out)

**Purpose:** To finalize payment, close the folio, and formally conclude the guest's stay.

**Required Materials:** PMS terminal, credit card terminal, printer.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Process final payment against the authorized card or collect outstanding cash balance.
2. Close the folio in the PMS.
3. Deactivate all issued room keys.
4. Update room status to "Vacant Dirty" for Housekeeping processing.
5. Provide a printed or emailed receipt as requested by the guest.
6. Ask about the guest's overall stay experience and document any feedback.
7. Offer assistance with luggage, transportation, or future booking.

**Quality Checkpoints:** Folio closed and keys deactivated within 3 minutes for standard checkouts.

**Expected Outcome:** Guest departs with an accurate final bill, deactivated keys, and a positive final interaction.

**Escalation Conditions:** Unresolved billing disputes or guest dissatisfaction expressed at checkout must be escalated to Supervisor or Duty Manager before the guest departs, if possible.

## 7. Best Practices

- **Always verify room status before issuing keys.** Sending a guest to an unready or still-occupied room creates a serious service failure and potential privacy breach for the previous guest.
- **Review the folio proactively, not defensively.** Walking the guest through charges before they ask builds trust and reduces disputes.
- **Confirm rate accuracy at the earliest possible point.** Catching a rate discrepancy during reservation retrieval, rather than at payment, prevents guest frustration and rebooking delays.
- **Treat express checkout guests with the same billing accuracy standard as in-person checkouts.** Guests still expect and deserve a fully reviewed, accurate folio even when they do not visit the desk.
- **Use key testing where available.** A malfunctioning key discovered at the desk saves the guest a return trip and protects first impressions.

## 8. Exceptions

**Early Check-In:** Granted when a room is verified "Vacant Inspected" ahead of standard check-in time; if unavailable, offer luggage storage and proactive notification once ready.

**Late Checkout / Extended Stay / Checkout Extension / Delayed Departure:** Agents may approve up to 1:00 PM based on occupancy; Supervisor approval required beyond that due to Housekeeping turnover impact on same-day arrivals.

**Walk-In Guests:** Verify current availability and rate before registration; walk-ins are subject to rack rate unless a promotional rate applies and is confirmed by the Supervisor.

**Group Check-In:** Pre-assigned rooms and pre-established billing per the group contract; verify master account versus individual guest incidentals before registration.

**VIP Check-In / Check-Out:** Expedited processing with pre-prepared key packets; checkout may include a personalized farewell coordinated with Guest Relations.

**Guests with Disabilities:** Accessible rooms must not be reassigned without guest consent; confirm accessibility features are functioning prior to check-in.

**System Failures (PMS Downtime):** Use manual registration cards and manual folio tracking; reconcile into the PMS once systems are restored.

## 9. Troubleshooting

**Problem: Guest's ID does not match the name on the reservation.**
- *Possible Causes:* Reservation made by a third party, name change, booking under a company name.
- *Resolution:* Confirm the guest's relationship to the reservation and authorization to check in; document accordingly.
- *Escalation:* If authorization cannot be confirmed, escalate to Front Office Supervisor.

**Problem: Incidental hold is declined at check-in.**
- *Possible Causes:* Insufficient available credit, incorrect card details.
- *Resolution:* Request an alternate payment method or reduce the incidental hold amount per property policy.
- *Escalation:* If no valid payment method is available, escalate to Supervisor.

**Problem: Guest requests late checkout, but room is needed for a same-day arrival.**
- *Possible Causes:* High occupancy night, limited room type inventory.
- *Resolution:* Offer the latest feasible time and luggage storage as an alternative.
- *Escalation:* Supervisor or Duty Manager approval required for any exception beyond standard authority.

**Problem: Room key fails to open the door after check-in.**
- *Possible Causes:* Demagnetized key, encoding error, door lock malfunction.
- *Resolution:* Re-encode the key; if failure persists, dispatch Maintenance to inspect the lock and offer an alternate room if needed.
- *Escalation:* Notify Maintenance immediately; notify Supervisor if guest requires room reassignment.

**Problem: Express checkout guest disputes a charge after departure.**
- *Possible Causes:* Unrecognized incidental charge, billing error.
- *Resolution:* Review the folio and supporting documentation; process a verified adjustment if applicable.
- *Escalation:* Escalate to Supervisor or Accounting for disputes exceeding Agent authority.

## 10. Frequently Asked Questions

**1. What identification is required at check-in?**
A valid, government-issued photo ID matching the name on the reservation is required for all check-ins.

**2. Can a guest check in if the reservation is under someone else's name?**
Only if the guest can demonstrate authorization (e.g., traveling companion, company booking); document the exception per property policy.

**3. What is the difference between an incidental hold and the room charge?**
The incidental hold is a temporary authorization to cover potential extra charges, while the room charge is the actual nightly rate billed to the folio.

**4. How do I process a walk-in guest?**
Confirm current availability, quote the applicable walk-in rate, and follow the standard check-in registration process.

**5. Can I check a guest out without them visiting the desk?**
Yes, via express checkout, provided the folio has been reviewed for accuracy and a valid payment method is on file.

**6. What if a guest wants to split payment between two cards?**
Process each payment method separately in the PMS folio, confirming the total charged matches the folio balance.

**7. How do I handle a guest requesting an early check-in with no rooms ready?**
Offer luggage storage, provide a realistic time estimate, and proactively contact the guest once a room becomes available.

**8. What is the checkout time, and how much can I extend it without approval?**
Standard checkout is 11:00 AM. Agents may extend up to 1:00 PM based on occupancy without Supervisor approval.

**9. What happens if a guest loses their key during their stay?**
Verify identity, deactivate the lost key immediately, and issue a new one.

**10. Can I waive the incidental hold for a loyalty-tier guest?**
Only if property policy explicitly permits this for the relevant loyalty tier; otherwise, standard hold procedures apply.

**11. What do I do if the guest's reservation shows a different rate than what they were quoted online?**
Escalate to the Supervisor to review the booking source and resolve the rate discrepancy before proceeding.

**12. How do I check in a group of guests arriving together?**
Refer to the group block in the PMS, confirm pre-assigned rooms and billing arrangement, and process each guest's registration individually unless a group registration process is specified.

**13. What should I do if a guest wants to check out but hasn't paid any incidental charges yet?**
Review the complete folio, collect payment for any outstanding incidental charges, then close the folio.

**14. Is a signature required for digital check-in via the PMS or mobile app?**
Yes, digital signature or acknowledgment is required to authorize charges, consistent with in-person registration requirements.

**15. Can I issue a key without ID verification if the guest is in a hurry?**
No. ID verification is mandatory for all check-ins regardless of guest time constraints.

**16. What if the room assigned doesn't match the guest's original request (e.g., wrong bed type)?**
Apologize, check for an available alternate room, and reassign if possible; if not, offer an appropriate service recovery gesture.

**17. How do I handle a returning guest who wants the same room as their last stay?**
Check availability of the specific room; if available and appropriate, assign it, noting the preference in the guest profile.

**18. What if a guest wants to extend their stay while already checked in?**
Confirm availability, update the reservation and folio accordingly, and reconfirm the new departure date with the guest.

**19. Do I need to reconfirm payment authorization if the guest extends their stay?**
Yes, verify and, if needed, update the incidental hold to reflect the extended stay duration.

**20. What is the correct process if the PMS goes down mid-check-in?**
Switch to manual registration procedures immediately, complete the transaction on paper, and enter all details into the PMS once it is restored.

## 13. Training Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Guest Arrives Without Photo ID
**Situation:** A guest arrives for check-in but has left their photo ID at home, offering only a credit card matching the reservation name.

**Correct Response:** Politely explain that photo ID is required per policy, and consult the Front Office Supervisor regarding any approved alternate verification method available at the property.

**Incorrect Response:** Checking the guest in without any identity verification because the credit card name matches.

**Supervisor Notes:** ID verification protects both the guest and the property; exceptions must always go through Supervisor approval, never Agent discretion alone.

**Learning Outcome:** Identification protocols exist for guest safety and fraud prevention and must be consistently enforced.

---

### Scenario 2: Guest Insists Their Quoted Rate Was Lower
**Situation:** A guest checking in insists their online booking confirmation showed a nightly rate $40 lower than what appears in the PMS.

**Correct Response:** Ask to see the confirmation if available, document the discrepancy, and escalate to the Supervisor for verification and resolution before finalizing payment.

**Incorrect Response:** Immediately adjusting the rate without verification, or dismissing the guest's claim without investigation.

**Supervisor Notes:** Rate discrepancies must always be verified through booking source records before any adjustment is made.

**Learning Outcome:** Rate accuracy issues require verification, documentation, and appropriate escalation, not unilateral Agent decisions.

---

### Scenario 3: Express Checkout Guest Calls to Dispute a Charge
**Situation:** A guest who used express checkout calls the next day disputing a $30 valet charge they don't recognize.

**Correct Response:** Review the folio and any supporting valet documentation, explain findings calmly, and process a verified adjustment if the charge cannot be substantiated.

**Incorrect Response:** Refusing to discuss the charge because the guest already checked out.

**Supervisor Notes:** Express checkout does not end the property's obligation to resolve legitimate billing questions.

**Learning Outcome:** Post-departure billing accuracy and guest service remain a Front Desk responsibility even after express checkout.
